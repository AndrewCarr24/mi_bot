# Enact Holdings Inc. (ACT)

> Enact Holdings, Inc. is a US private mortgage insurer that was carved out of Genworth Financial in 2021 via an IPO, though Genworth continues to beneficially own at least 80% of Enact's common stock. Enact operates a single-segment mortgage insurance business through its primary subsidiary Enact Mortgage Insurance Corporation (EMICO), with a Bermuda-based reinsurance subsidiary (Enact Re Ltd.) that provides intercompany quota share reinsurance and participates in GSE credit risk transfer transactions. The company has delivered strong financial performance in recent years, with record adjusted operating income in 2024 and consistent capital return to shareholders through buybacks and dividends.

## What it is

Enact Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACT) is a mortgage insurance holding company whose primary operating subsidiary is Enact Mortgage Insurance Corporation. The company writes private mortgage insurance (PMI) on residential loans originated by US lenders, protecting lenders and the GSEs (Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac) against borrower default. Enact was formed through the separation of Genworth Financial's US mortgage insurance business and completed its IPO in September 2021.

Genworth continues to exert significant influence over Enact, controlling all matters requiring a stockholder vote, including the election of directors and decisions affecting capital structure (ACT_10-K_2024-12-31). The two companies operate under a Master Agreement, a Shared Services Agreement, and other intercompany arrangements. Genworth provides Enact with investment management, information technology, and administrative services (ACT_10-K_2024-12-31).

Enact operates in a single reportable segment — Mortgage Insurance — and its chief operating decision maker (the CEO) evaluates performance on consolidated net income (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Enact also owns Enact Re Ltd. ("Enact Re"), a wholly owned Bermuda-based subsidiary whose primary function is to reinsure EMICO's new and existing insurance-in-force under quota share reinsurance agreements; Enact Re also participates in GSE credit risk transfer transactions (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Enact contributed $500 million of capital into Enact Re in 2023 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31), and management views the platform as a long-term, capital- and expense-efficient growth opportunity (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

## Why it matters

Enact is one of the six active US private mortgage insurers and is distinguished by its ongoing relationship with Genworth, which creates both structural advantages (access to shared services, established infrastructure) and governance complexities (concentrated ownership, potential conflicts of interest). The company's capital return profile is among the more aggressive in the industry — since its IPO, Enact has returned over $1.1 billion to shareholders through buybacks and dividends (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

Analysts watch Enact for its credit performance as its book seasons (average age ~3.8 years as of late 2024), its ability to grow insurance-in-force in a high-rate environment, and its capital allocation decisions, which are subject to regulatory dividend restrictions at the insurance subsidiary level (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Enact's PMIERs sufficiency was 162% at December 31, 2025 ($5,015M Available Assets vs $3,096M required, $1,919M cushion), down from 167% / $2,052M cushion at year-end 2024 — still well above the 100% floor, providing substantial capital flexibility (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

Based on Enact's 2025 Form 10-K, the company's most recent full-year financials show:

- **Total revenues**: $1,236 million (2025), up from $1,202 million (2024) and $1,154 million (2023)
- **Net income**: $674 million (2025), compared to $688 million (2024) and $666 million (2023)
- **Diluted EPS**: $4.52 (2025), $4.37 (2024), $4.11 (2023)
- **NIW**: $51.5 billion (2025), up modestly from $51.0 billion (2024) and down from $53.1 billion (2023)

- **PMIERs sufficiency**: 162% at December 31, 2025 ($5,015M Available Assets, $1,919M cushion above the $3,096M Minimum Required Assets), compared to 167% / $2,052M cushion at year-end 2024

(ACT_10-K_2025-12-31)

The company's investment portfolio totaled $6,051 million at fair value as of December 31, 2025, primarily in fixed maturity securities including US corporates, asset-backed securities, and municipal bonds (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Loss reserves stood at $572 million.

## How it has evolved

Enact's financial trajectory since its 2021 IPO has been characterized by steady premium growth, improving investment income, and aggressive capital return. Premiums grew from $939 million in 2022 to $981 million in 2025, reflecting growth in insurance-in-force (ACT_10-K_2024-12-31; ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Net investment income rose from $155 million in 2022 to $266 million in 2025, benefiting from higher interest rates and portfolio growth.

The company's share count has declined meaningfully through buybacks: weighted average diluted shares fell from 163.3 million in 2022 to 149.3 million in 2025 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). In 2024, Enact issued $750 million in senior notes — its first investment-grade debt issuance as a public company — and used proceeds partly to repay existing debt, generating $2 million in annual interest savings (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

Enact's credit performance has remained benign through 2023-2025, with losses incurred of $110 million in 2025 versus $39 million in 2024 and $27 million in 2023 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). The company's book continues to season, with the average age reaching 3.8 years by Q4 2024, approaching the plateau of normal delinquency development (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

The Enact Re Bermuda reinsurance platform was scaled up materially in 2023, when Enact contributed $500 million of capital to support its quota share reinsurance of EMICO and its participation in GSE credit risk transfer transactions (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). At the parent-company level, Enact Holdings was upgraded by S&P from BBB+ to A- (issuer credit rating) in 2024, reflecting the strengthened balance sheet (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). Management views the Enact Re platform as a long-term, capital- and expense-efficient growth opportunity that could expand further if GSE risk-sharing volumes increase (ACT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).
