# MGIC Investment Corporation (MTG)

> MGIC Investment Corporation, through its subsidiary Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation (MGIC), is the largest private mortgage insurer in the United States by market share. The company provides primary mortgage insurance on low down payment residential loans, protecting lenders and the GSEs against default losses. As of year-end 2025, MGIC held $303.1 billion in direct primary insurance in force and generated $738.3 million in net income for the year, supported by favorable credit trends and disciplined capital management. The company has consistently returned substantial capital to shareholders through share repurchases and dividends, maintaining a payout ratio near or above 90% of net income.

## What it is

MGIC Investment Corporation (NYSE: MTG) is a holding company whose primary operating subsidiary, Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation, underwrites private mortgage insurance on residential first-lien loans originated by lenders across the United States. MGIC also operates MGIC Assurance Corporation (MAC) and MGIC Indemnity Corporation (MIC), which provide insurance for certain mortgages under GSE credit risk transfer programs. The company operates as a single reporting segment (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

MGIC's core product is primary mortgage insurance — coverage that protects the lender or GSE against a portion of the loss if a borrower defaults. The insurance covers a percentage of the unpaid loan principal, delinquent interest, and certain foreclosure-related expenses. The substantial majority of MGIC's new insurance written is on loans purchased by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

The company uses a risk-based pricing engine called MiQ, which allows frequent granular pricing adjustments to address evolving market conditions and risk views (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). MGIC distributes its products through a direct sales force covering the United States, Puerto Rico, and Guam.

## Why it matters

MGIC is the largest player in a six-company oligopoly that dominates the US private mortgage insurance market. MGIC's estimated share of PMI industry NIW was 18.6% in 2024, up from 16.3% in 2023 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31), giving it scale advantages in risk diversification, reinsurance execution, and customer relationships. For analysts, MGIC is a bellwether for industry trends: its NIW volumes, persistency rates, and credit quality shifts signal broader market conditions.

The company's capital return story is a key differentiator. MGIC returned approximately $700 million to shareholders in 2024 — a 92% payout ratio — and has made share repurchases the primary means of returning capital (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). This positions MGIC as a high-yielding capital return story within the financial sector, but it also means the stock is sensitive to any deterioration in the credit cycle that would force capital conservation.

MGIC's exposure to the FHA competitive dynamic is material. The FHA's 30-basis-point premium rate reduction in 2023 negatively impacted MGIC's NIW, and the PMI industry's share of total primary mortgage insurance volume declined from 44.1% in 2023 to 41.1% in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). Any further FHA pricing actions would directly affect MGIC's growth trajectory.

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025, MGIC reported:

- **Net income**: $738.3 million, down from $763.0 million in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Adjusted net operating income**: $738.4 million, or $3.14 per diluted share (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Net premiums earned**: $965.8 million, versus $970.8 million in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Direct primary IIF**: $303.1 billion (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Direct primary RIF**: $81.2 billion (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **NIW**: $60.2 billion, up from $55.7 billion in 2024, reflecting higher expected market position (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Losses incurred, net**: $48.9 million (a net expense), compared to a benefit of $14.9 million in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Shareholders' equity**: $5.15 billion, with 219.4 million shares outstanding (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **PMIERs compliance**: MGIC's Available Assets exceeded Minimum Required Assets; the company does not expect the August 2024 PMIERs update to affect its compliance (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31)

Credit quality of NIW remained strong: 51.7% of 2025 NIW had FICO scores of 760+, 14.7% had LTVs above 95%, and 27.0% had DTI ratios above 45%. Purchase mortgages accounted for 90.9% of NIW, with refinances at 9.1% (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

MGIC emerged from the 2008 financial crisis with significant losses on its 2005-2008 book, but the post-crisis regulatory framework (PMIERs) and conservative underwriting have transformed the company. Since 2020, MGIC has benefited from a prolonged period of favorable credit: the COVID-era delinquency spike reversed quickly, and loss reserves have been released in each of the last several years (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

Net income has been consistently strong: $865 million in 2022, $713 million in 2023, $763 million in 2024, and $738 million in 2025 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). The slight decline in 2025 reflects a normalization of loss experience from the unusually benign levels of 2023-2024.

Capital return has accelerated dramatically. In 2024, MGIC repurchased shares aggressively, reducing the share count from 272.5 million (year-end 2023) to 248.4 million (year-end 2024), and further to 219.4 million by year-end 2025 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). The company retired all treasury stock in 2024, simplifying its capital structure.

Market share has trended upward: MGIC's estimated share of PMI industry NIW rose from 16.3% in 2023 to 18.6% in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). The company's top 10 customers generated 37% of NIW in both 2024 and 2023, indicating stable customer concentration (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).
