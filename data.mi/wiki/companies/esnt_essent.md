# Essent Group Ltd. (ESNT)

> Essent Group Ltd. is a Bermuda-based holding company that provides private mortgage insurance and reinsurance in the U.S. through its primary operating subsidiaries, Essent Guaranty, Inc. and Essent Reinsurance Ltd. The company has built a reputation for conservative underwriting, disciplined capital management, and a "Buy, Manage & Distribute" operating model that relies heavily on programmatic reinsurance. As of year-end 2025, Essent held $248.4 billion of mortgage insurance in force, maintained a PMIERs sufficiency ratio of 178%, and generated a 14% return on average equity in 2024.

## What it is

Essent Group Ltd. (NYSE: ESNT) is a Bermuda-domiciled holding company that operates through two reportable segments: Mortgage Insurance and Reinsurance (as of the fourth quarter of 2025, when the company recast its segment reporting to separate third-party reinsurance from mortgage insurance) (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). Its U.S. mortgage insurance subsidiary, Essent Guaranty, Inc., is a Pennsylvania-domiciled monoline mortgage guaranty insurer licensed in all 50 states and the District of Columbia, and approved by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. Essent Guaranty writes private mortgage insurance on residential first-lien loans with down payments of less than 20%, satisfying the GSEs' credit protection requirements (ESNT_10-K_2024-12-31).

Essent Reinsurance Ltd. (Essent Re), a Bermuda-based Class 3B insurer, reinsures a portion of Essent Guaranty's new insurance written (NIW) under a quota share agreement — 50% for NIW from January 2025 onward, up from 35% for 2021-2024 and 25% prior to 2021 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). Essent Re also writes GSE credit risk transfer business and, beginning in 2026, will reinsure certain property and casualty risks through the Lloyd's of London market. Additionally, Essent has a title insurance operation (Essent Title Insurance, Inc.) acquired in July 2023, which is included in "Corporate and Other" (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Essent Guaranty carries financial strength ratings of A3 (positive outlook) from Moody's, A- (stable) from S&P, and A (Excellent, stable) from A.M. Best (ESNT_10-K_2024-12-31).

## Why it matters

Essent is the third-largest U.S. private mortgage insurer by market share and is often viewed by analysts as the most conservatively managed of the six MIs. Its "Buy, Manage & Distribute" model — ceding a meaningful portion of mezzanine credit risk through programmatic quota share reinsurance — reduces earnings volatility and capital intensity relative to peers. As of year-end 2024, approximately 97% of Essent's portfolio was covered by some form of reinsurance (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

The company's Bermuda domicile provides a tax advantage: Essent Group and Essent Re are not subject to U.S. corporate income tax, though a Bermuda corporate income tax is under legislative consideration (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). This structure, combined with the quota share arrangement, means that a significant portion of underwriting profit flows to the Bermuda reinsurer, reducing the effective tax rate on the consolidated entity.

Essent's credit quality is among the strongest in the industry. As of December 31, 2024, its in-force portfolio had a weighted average FICO of 746 and a weighted average original LTV of 93% (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). The company prices new business assuming a combined ratio of roughly 35% to 45%, reflecting its expectation of very low loss content on new originations (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025, Essent reported net income of $690 million ($6.90 per diluted share), compared to $729 million ($6.85 per diluted share) in 2024 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). The decline was driven by a higher provision for losses and LAE — $149 million in 2025 versus $81 million in 2024 — as the portfolio aged and defaults normalized from historically low levels.

Key metrics as of December 31, 2025:
- **Insurance in Force (IIF):** $248.4 billion, up 2% from $243.6 billion at year-end 2024 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **NIW:** $46.6 billion in 2025, compared to $45.6 billion in 2024 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Risk in Force (RIF):** $56.5 billion (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Average net premium rate:** 0.35% (unchanged from 2024) (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **PMIERs sufficiency ratio:** 178% as of December 31, 2024 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31)
- **Book value per share:** $53.36 as of December 31, 2024, up 11% year-over-year (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31)
- **GAAP equity:** $5.8 billion as of December 31, 2025 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31)
- **Persistency:** 86% as of December 31, 2024, with nearly 60% of the in-force portfolio having a note rate of 5.5% or lower (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31)

Essent Re generated approximately $80 million in third-party revenues in 2024 and had $2.2 billion in third-party risk in force (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). Since 2014, Essent Re has earned over $450 million of net income from its third-party business and contributed approximately $800 million to Essent's book value (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

The company's investment portfolio totaled $6.5 billion at fair value as of December 31, 2025, with a new money yield over 5% in the fourth quarter of 2024 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). Net investment income was $237 million in 2025, up from $222 million in 2024 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Capital management: In early 2025, the Board approved an 11% increase in the quarterly dividend to $0.31 per share and a $500 million share repurchase authorization through year-end 2026 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). The company repurchased $588 million of stock in 2025 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

Essent was founded in 2008 and began writing mortgage insurance in 2009, entering the market after the Global Financial Crisis had eliminated several legacy MIs. The company grew rapidly through the 2010s, benefiting from strong housing fundamentals and a disciplined underwriting approach.

The quota share reinsurance arrangement with Essent Re has been a defining feature. The ceding percentage increased from 25% (pre-2021) to 35% (2021-2024) and then to 50% effective January 2025, reflecting Essent's increasing reliance on third-party capital to manage risk and improve capital efficiency (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Essent's IIF grew from $227 billion at the end of 2023 to $239 billion at the end of 2024 and $248 billion at the end of 2025, a compound annual growth rate of roughly 4.5% (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). This growth has been constrained by elevated mortgage rates suppressing refinance activity and limiting new originations — persistency remained elevated at 86% as of late 2024, as borrowers with low note rates stayed in place (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

The company expanded into title insurance in July 2023 with the acquisitions of Agents National Title Insurance Company and Boston National Title, though this segment has been a drag on earnings, incurring a pre-tax loss of approximately $21 million in 2024 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31). Management maintains a long-term view for the title business but does not expect material near-term earnings contribution.

In the fourth quarter of 2025, Essent Re entered the Lloyd's of London market to reinsure property and casualty risks, marking a strategic expansion beyond mortgage credit risk (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). This diversification reflects management's ambition to build a multi-line reinsurance platform.
