# Arch Capital Group Ltd. (ACGL)

> Arch Capital Group Ltd. is a Bermuda-based global insurer and reinsurer operating through three segments: insurance, reinsurance, and mortgage. The mortgage segment provides U.S. private mortgage insurance (through Arch MI U.S.), international mortgage insurance and reinsurance, and participation in GSE credit risk-sharing transactions. Arch is one of the six major U.S. mortgage insurers and is unique among them in that mortgage insurance is one of three diversified underwriting segments rather than the sole business. At year-end 2025 the group reported approximately $26.9 billion in capital and book value per share of $65.11 (a 22.6% increase from $53.11 at year-end 2024); for full-year 2025 Arch wrote $16.5 billion of net premiums and reported $4.4 billion of net income available to common shareholders (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

## What it is

Arch Capital Group Ltd. (ACGL) is a publicly traded Bermuda exempted company that provides insurance, reinsurance, and mortgage insurance worldwide through its wholly owned subsidiaries. The company was formed in September 2000 and launched its underwriting initiative in October 2001 with a $763.2 million capital infusion (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31).

The **mortgage segment** includes:

- **U.S. direct mortgage insurance** — primarily through Arch Mortgage Insurance Company (AMIC), United Guaranty Residential Insurance Company (UGRIC), and Arch Mortgage Guaranty Company (AMG). AMIC and UGRIC are approved as eligible mortgage insurers by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. AMG insures mortgages not intended for sale to the GSEs (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **International mortgage insurance and reinsurance** — through Arch Re Bermuda (proportional and non-proportional), Arch Insurance (EU) in the EEA and U.K., and Arch Indemnity in Australia (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **GSE credit risk-sharing transactions** — primarily through Arch Re Bermuda (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

Arch entered the U.S. mortgage insurance market in 2014 on the AMIC platform and expanded significantly with the December 2016 acquisition of UGC and its subsidiary UGRIC (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31). The mortgage segment's strategy emphasizes disciplined credit risk analysis, maintaining a leading U.S. market position, and diversifying revenues through international opportunities (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Arch is distinctive among the six U.S. mortgage insurers because its mortgage segment operates within a diversified insurance and reinsurance group. This structure provides several advantages:

- **Capital flexibility** — The mortgage segment can draw on the broader group's capital base, and the group can allocate capital across insurance, reinsurance, and mortgage based on relative risk-adjusted returns (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31).
- **Diversified revenue stream** — The mortgage market operates on a distinct underwriting cycle from property/casualty lines, providing earnings diversification (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31).
- **Cross-segment expertise** — Arch's reinsurance operations support the mortgage group's participation in GSE credit risk-sharing transactions and international mortgage reinsurance (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

For analysts, Arch's mortgage segment performance must be evaluated both on its own merits (NIW, persistency, loss ratio, PMIERs compliance) and in the context of the group's overall underwriting results. The mortgage segment has historically been a strong contributor to group underwriting income — for example, in 2023 the mortgage segment generated $1,064 million in underwriting income with a negative loss ratio (i.e., favorable prior-period reserve releases exceeded the current-period provision for new losses), compared to $450 million for insurance and $1,098 million for reinsurance (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

Based on Arch's 2025 Form 10-K, the mortgage segment reported:

| Metric | 2025 | 2024 | 2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gross premiums written | $1,305M | $1,351M | $1,387M |
| Net premiums written | $1,060M | $1,112M | $1,052M |
| Net premiums earned | $1,172M | $1,231M | $1,158M |
| Underwriting income | $1,000M | $1,094M | $1,064M |
| Loss ratio | -0.4% | -4.4% | -8.9% |
| Combined ratio | 14.6% | 12.6% | 9.3% |

The 2025 result marks the fourth consecutive year of mortgage-segment underwriting income above $1 billion. The trend in the loss and combined ratios — moving from a deeply negative loss ratio of -8.9% in 2023 toward -0.4% in 2025 — reflects normal aging of the in-force book as favorable prior-year reserve development moderates from the post-COVID peak (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

At the group level, Arch reported approximately $26.9 billion in capital at December 31, 2025, with book value per share of $65.11 — a 22.6% increase from $53.11 at year-end 2024 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). For full-year 2025, Arch wrote $16.5 billion of net premiums and reported $4.4 billion of net income available to common shareholders (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). Capital return shifted from a $1.9 billion special dividend in 2024 to $1.9 billion of share repurchases in 2025 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). The U.S. primary mortgage insurance persistency rate was 81.8% at December 31, 2025, compared to 82.1% at year-end 2024 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

On the Q4 2024 earnings call, management noted that the mortgage insurance market conditions remained favorable, with the company selectively deploying capital to areas producing attractive risk-adjusted returns (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

## How it has evolved

Arch's mortgage segment has grown from a greenfield entry in 2014 to one of the top U.S. mortgage insurers through the 2016 UGC acquisition and organic expansion. Key milestones:

- **2014** — Entered U.S. mortgage insurance market on the AMIC platform (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31).
- **2016** — Acquired UGC and UGRIC, significantly expanding U.S. market share (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31).
- **2019** — Arch LMI authorized by APRA to write lenders' mortgage insurance in Australia (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **2021** — Acquired Arch Indemnity, expanding Australian mortgage insurance operations (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **2022** — Converted Arch LMI to a services company; Arch Indemnity became the primary Australian mortgage insurance provider (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **2024** — Acquired Allianz's U.S. Middle Market P&C business (MCE Acquisition), expanding the insurance segment; the mortgage segment continued to benefit from favorable credit conditions (ACGL_10-K_2024-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2024-12-31).

The mortgage segment's underwriting income has been consistently strong, with negative loss ratios in recent years reflecting favorable credit performance and reserve releases. The segment's combined ratio of 9.3% in 2023 (i.e., underwriting profit of 90.7 points) highlights the profitability of the mortgage insurance book during benign credit conditions (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
