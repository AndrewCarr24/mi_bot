# Reinsurance and Credit Risk Transfer (CRT)

> Reinsurance is a core capital management tool for private mortgage insurers, allowing them to transfer a portion of their insured risk to third-party reinsurers or capital markets investors. The industry uses a combination of quota share reinsurance (QSR), excess-of-loss (XOL) reinsurance, and insurance-linked notes (ILNs) to reduce PMIERs capital requirements, manage loss volatility, and improve return on equity. Since 2015, the MI industry has transformed from a "buy-and-hold" model to an "aggregate-manage-distribute" model, transferring nearly $73.8 billion in risk on over $3.4 trillion of insurance-in-force through 2023 (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

## What it is

Reinsurance in the MI context refers to arrangements where a mortgage insurer cedes a portion of its insured risk to other parties in exchange for a share of premiums. There are three primary structures:

**Quota Share Reinsurance (QSR):** The MI cedes a fixed percentage of premiums and losses on a defined block of policies to a panel of third-party reinsurers. The MI typically receives a ceding commission (e.g., 20% of ceded premiums at MGIC) and a profit commission that varies inversely with loss experience (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). QSR contracts often have annual loss ratio caps (commonly 300%) and lifetime loss ratio caps (commonly 200%) (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

**Excess-of-Loss (XOL) Reinsurance:** The MI retains a first-loss layer (typically 185-250 basis points of risk) and transfers excess layers to either traditional reinsurers or capital markets investors via ILNs (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11). ILNs are non-recourse notes issued by special-purpose entities, with proceeds held in trust to fund future reinsurance claims (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

**MI Credit Risk Transfer (MI-CRT):** An umbrella term encompassing both QSR and ILN/XOL structures. Since 2015, the industry has executed 53 QSR/XOL deals ceding $51.5 billion of risk and 56 ILN transactions transferring $22.3 billion of risk (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

## Why it matters

Reinsurance serves multiple strategic purposes for MIs and is closely watched by analysts:

**PMIERs capital relief:** Reinsurance directly reduces an MI's Minimum Required Assets under PMIERs. For example, Radian Guaranty's reinsurance programs reduced its Minimum Required Assets by $1.46 billion as of December 31, 2024, representing 27.4% of gross Minimum Required Assets (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31). This capital relief is a key driver of PMIERs cushion levels and the ability to write new business.

**Loss volatility management:** By transferring tail risk, MIs reduce the earnings impact of severe loss events. The industry's shift to an "aggregate-manage-distribute" model means MIs act as sophisticated risk managers rather than passive holders (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

**Return on equity enhancement:** Reinsurance can be accretive to returns by freeing up capital at a fraction of the cost of equity capital, without adding financial leverage (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

**Counterparty risk:** Reinsurance does not relieve the MI of its direct liability to policyholders. If a reinsurer fails to pay, the MI remains obligated (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31). This creates counterparty credit risk that analysts must monitor.

**Regulatory risk:** The PMIERs credit received for reinsurance is subject to GSE review and could be reduced if the GSEs align PMIERs with the Enterprise Regulatory Capital Framework (ERCF), which provides reduced credit for GSE CRT activities (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

All six MIs actively use reinsurance, though structures and scale vary:

**MGIC:** As of December 31, 2025, MGIC had $20.6 billion in total ceded risk-in-force, up from $18.0 billion at year-end 2024. The book is spread across QSR transactions for policy years 2021-2025 plus the specialized Credit Union QSR (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Cede rates have stepped up materially — the 2024 QSR ceded 30% (subsequently amended down to 28% effective December 31, 2025) and the 2025 QSR cedes 40%; MGIC has also executed two further 40% QSR transactions covering NIW in 2026 and 2027 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). The Credit Union QSR has been at 65% since 2020. On the ILN side, MGIC's active Home Re transactions at year-end 2025 are 2021-2, 2022-1, and 2023-1; Home Re 2021-1 was terminated in 2025 following the 2024 terminations of Home Re 2018-1, 2019-1, and 2020-1 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). MGIC's risk-to-capital ratio was 10.0:1 at year-end 2025, well below the 25:1 statutory cap.

**Radian:** Radian Guaranty's reinsurance programs reduced PMIERs Minimum Required Assets by $1.78 billion as of December 31, 2025, equal to 31.8% of gross MRA — up from $1.46 billion (27.4%) at year-end 2024 (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31). The 2025 breakdown: $913.2 million from the QSR Program, $479.5 million from traditional XOL reinsurance, and $389.0 million from the mortgage insurance-linked notes (Eagle Re) program (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

**Industry-wide:** The most recent industry-level disclosure is USMI's November 2023 resiliency paper, which reported that since 2015 the industry had transferred nearly $73.8 billion in risk on more than $3.4 trillion of insurance-in-force, split roughly 70% traditional reinsurance ($51.5 billion across 53 QSR/XOL deals) and 30% capital-markets ILNs ($22.3 billion across 56 transactions) (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11). The cumulative figures will be higher as of late 2025 given the continued pace of QSR and ILN activity at the major MIs, but no updated industry aggregate is in the corpus.

## How it has evolved

**Pre-2008:** MIs operated primarily on a "buy-and-hold" model, retaining virtually all risk on their balance sheets. This left them vulnerable to severe loss cycles.

**Post-2008 transformation:** The aligned PMIERs framework — drafted in 2014, effective in 2015, then refined as PMIERs 2.0 effective March 31, 2019 (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE) — created explicit capital incentives for risk distribution. MIs built reinsurance programs to manage PMIERs Minimum Required Assets and improve return on equity.

**2015 inflection point:** The industry launched its first ILN programs, accessing capital markets investors. This marked the beginning of the "aggregate-manage-distribute" model (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

**2019-2025 maturation:** QSR programs expanded in scale and complexity. MGIC's broad-program cede rates ran 10-15% in 2021-2023 before stepping up to 30% on the 2024 QSR and 40% on the 2025 QSR (and on follow-on 40% QSRs for 2026 and 2027 NIW); the specialized Credit Union QSR has run at 65% since 2020 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Radian's PMIERs benefit from reinsurance grew from $1.14 billion in 2022 to $1.60 billion in 2023, eased to $1.46 billion in 2024, then climbed to $1.78 billion (31.8% of gross MRA) at year-end 2025 (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2024-12-31). The industry has also demonstrated the ability to terminate and replace older ILN structures as market conditions evolve — MGIC, for instance, has retired four Home Re transactions (2018-1, 2019-1, 2020-1, 2021-1) across 2024 and 2025.
