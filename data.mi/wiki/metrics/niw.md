# New Insurance Written (NIW)

> New Insurance Written (NIW) is the dollar volume of new primary mortgage insurance policies issued in a given period. It is the primary top-line volume metric for mortgage insurers, directly driving future premium revenue and insurance-in-force growth. NIW is a function of three variables: total mortgage origination volume, the share of originations that require private mortgage insurance (PMI penetration), and the insurer's market share of the PMI market. Analysts track NIW as a leading indicator of future earned premiums and as a measure of competitive positioning.

## What it is

NIW represents the aggregate unpaid principal balance of all new mortgage insurance policies written during a reporting period, typically measured in billions of dollars. For each mortgage insurer, NIW is the gross addition to its insurance-in-force (IIF) before cancellations, principal payments, and other reductions. The metric is reported on a direct basis (before reinsurance cessions) and is broken down by payment type (monthly, single, annual premiums) and by loan purpose (purchase vs. refinance) (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

The volume of NIW is influenced by three primary drivers (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31):

1. **Total mortgage origination market size** — driven by interest rates, home prices, housing supply, demographic trends, and economic conditions
2. **PMI penetration rate** — the percentage of mortgage originations that use private mortgage insurance rather than FHA, VA, or other credit enhancement; purchase loans have higher penetration than refinances because they typically have lower down payments
3. **Market share** — each insurer's share of the total PMI market, influenced by pricing, underwriting guidelines, customer relationships, and service quality

## Why it matters

NIW is the engine of future premium revenue for mortgage insurers. Each dollar of NIW contributes to the insurer's insurance-in-force, which generates premium income over the life of the policy. Because mortgage insurance policies typically have multi-year lives (with persistency rates currently around 84-86%), the NIW written in any given year will contribute premiums for several years to come (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

For analysts, NIW trends reveal:
- **Competitive dynamics** — shifts in market share among the six MIs signal pricing aggressiveness, underwriting appetite, or customer relationship changes
- **Portfolio quality** — the credit characteristics of NIW (FICO scores, LTV ratios, DTI ratios) determine the risk profile of the growing book; loans with higher LTVs or lower FICOs require more capital under PMIERs (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31)
- **Cyclical positioning** — NIW tends to rise when interest rates decline (boosting refinance volumes) and when home prices rise (increasing purchase loan sizes); conversely, rising rates suppress both purchase and refinance activity
- **Premium yield trajectory** — the mix of monthly vs. single premium policies in NIW affects the timing and yield of premium recognition; single premium policies generate upfront revenue but shorter earning periods

## Current state (as of 2024-12-31)

Based on the most recent annual filings:

- **MGIC** wrote $55.7 billion in primary NIW in 2024, up from $46.1 billion in 2023. Purchase loans represented 96.0% of NIW, refinances 4.0%. Monthly premium policies accounted for 97.6% of NIW, single premiums 2.4% (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

- **Radian** wrote $52.0 billion in first-lien primary NIW in 2024, compared to $52.7 billion in 2023. Radian's estimated share of the private mortgage insurance market was approximately 17% for 2024 (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31).

The total private mortgage insurance market was approximately $300 billion in 2024, with industry projections estimating ~$340 billion for 2025 (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31). PMI penetration of the overall mortgage origination market averaged approximately 16-18% in 2024, with purchase penetration around 22-23% and refinance penetration around 2-4% (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31).

## How it has evolved

NIW has been highly cyclical over the past five years, driven primarily by interest rate movements:

- **2020-2021**: Record-low mortgage rates drove a massive refinance boom. MGIC's NIW rose from $38.0 billion in 2020 to $69.8 billion in 2021 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

- **2022**: NIW reached its cycle peak before the rate-hiking cycle bit. MGIC wrote $76.4 billion in 2022 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). The Federal Reserve's rate hikes that year sharply reduced refinance volumes and purchase originations also declined as affordability deteriorated, setting up the contraction in subsequent years.

- **2023**: NIW remained depressed as rates stayed elevated. Purchase originations stabilized at lower levels, while refinance activity was minimal. MGIC's NIW fell to $46.1 billion (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).

- **2024**: A modest recovery driven by a brief decline in interest rates that boosted refinance volumes. Purchase activity remained relatively flat. MGIC's NIW recovered to $55.7 billion, while Radian's was essentially flat at $52.0 billion (RDN_10-K_2024-12-31).

The composition of NIW has shifted markedly: purchase loans now dominate (96%+ of NIW for MGIC in 2024), compared to the refinance-heavy mix of 2020-2021. This shift is positive for credit quality (purchase loans have lower LTVs on average) but means NIW is now more dependent on housing market fundamentals than on interest rate movements (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31).
