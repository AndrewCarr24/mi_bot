# MI Regulatory Landscape

> The U.S. private mortgage insurance industry is regulated through three concurrent layers — state insurance departments at each MI's domicile, the Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac PMIERs framework, and federal consumer-protection and capital-markets statutes — plus, for the four MIs with Bermuda subsidiaries (Arch, Essent, Enact, MGIC's Home Re Entities), the Bermuda Monetary Authority. State regulation governs solvency (risk-to-capital ratios, statutory contingency reserves), product approval, and dividend capacity; PMIERs governs eligibility to write loans for the GSEs; and federal law (notably the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 and Dodd-Frank's QM/QRM framework) shapes coverage cancellation rules and lender economics. State of domicile drives the primary regulator: MGIC and NMI's NMIC are Wisconsin-domiciled (Wisconsin OCI); Radian Guaranty and Essent Guaranty are Pennsylvania-domiciled (Pennsylvania Insurance Department); Enact's EMICO is North Carolina-domiciled (North Carolina Department of Insurance) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## What it is

**State regulation** is the ground floor. Each U.S. mortgage insurance subsidiary is domiciled in one state (and primarily regulated by that state's insurance department), and is licensed in every other state in which it writes business. Domicile-by-MI:

- **MGIC** (Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation) — Wisconsin; primary regulator is the Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance ("OCI") (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **NMIC** (National Mortgage Insurance Corporation) and **Re One** — Wisconsin; primary regulator is the Wisconsin OCI (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Radian Guaranty Inc.** — Pennsylvania; primary regulator is the Pennsylvania Insurance Department (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Essent Guaranty, Inc.** — Pennsylvania-domiciled monoline mortgage guaranty insurer; primary regulator is the Pennsylvania Insurance Department (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Enact Mortgage Insurance Corporation ("EMICO")** — North Carolina; primary regulator is the North Carolina Department of Insurance ("NCDOI") (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Arch Mortgage Insurance Company** — operates within Arch's broader subsidiary structure; subject to state insurance regulation in its domicile and licensing jurisdictions (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

Common state-level requirements:

- **Risk-to-capital ("RTC") ratio limit**: Most state MI regulations cap the RTC ratio at 25:1 (RIF, net of reinsurance and excluding policies in default with established loss reserves, divided by statutory capital). At year-end 2025, EMICO's RTC was 10.1:1 (NC limit 25:1) (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31), and Essent Guaranty's was 9.1:1 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Wisconsin uses a formula-based limit "slightly higher" than the standard 25:1 (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **GSE-imposed RTC ratio for reinsurance credit**: The GSEs require approved insurers' RTC ratio not to exceed 18:1 (a tighter limit than the 25:1 state ceiling), or they reserve the right to reevaluate the amount of PMIERs credit for reinsurance and other CRT transactions (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Statutory contingency reserve**: All state MI regulations require an MI to establish a special statutory contingency reserve, with annual contributions equal to at least 50% of net earned premiums; reserves are held for 10 years before they can be released, except that with regulator approval an MI may make early withdrawals when incurred losses exceed 35% of net premiums earned in a calendar year (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31). Examples: Enact's mortgage-insurance subsidiaries' statutory contingency reserve was $4,513M at YE2025 (vs. $4,336M at YE2024) (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31); Essent Guaranty's was $2,600M at YE2025 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **Dividend restrictions**: An MI's ability to pay dividends to its parent is constrained by domicile state law. In Wisconsin, "ordinary" dividends are capped at the lesser of 10% of statutory policyholders' surplus or adjusted statutory net income; "extraordinary" dividends require the OCI's prior approval (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31). Pennsylvania and North Carolina apply similar but distinct frameworks.

**PMIERs (the GSE eligibility framework)** sits on top of state regulation. PMIERs is administered by the GSEs at the direction of the FHFA and requires Available Assets to meet or exceed Minimum Required Assets (a risk-based asset amount based on RIF and a battery of risk-factor tables, with reinsurance credit and a $400M floor) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). See [[pmiers_aug_2024_update]] for the August 2024 phase-in.

**Federal regulation** governs the consumer-facing and lender-facing aspects of MI:

- **Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 ("HOPA")**: Requires automatic termination of borrower-paid mortgage insurance when the LTV (based on original property value and amortized loan balance) reaches 78%; provides for cancellation upon borrower request when LTV reaches 80% with a "good payment history" (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **CFPB ATR/QM rules and QRM Rule (Dodd-Frank)**: The Qualified Mortgage rule and the Qualified Residential Mortgage Rule shape lender economics on which loans qualify for risk-retention exemptions; the QRM definition currently aligns with QM (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **NAIC Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Model Act (revised 2023)**: In August 2023 the NAIC adopted a revised Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Model Act covering capital and minimum capital requirements, contingency reserves, investment restrictions, prudent underwriting standards, formal underwriting guidelines, internal Mortgage Guaranty Quality Control Programs, and reinsurance / captive reinsurance prohibitions. As of year-end 2025, no jurisdiction had formally adopted it; Wisconsin "has begun the process" of adoption with modifications expected (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**Bermuda regulation** applies to the Bermuda subsidiaries (Essent Re, Enact Re, Arch's Bermuda operating companies, and MGIC's Home Re Entities). Essent Re and Enact Re are both Class 3B insurers regulated by the Bermuda Monetary Authority ("BMA") under the Bermuda Insurance Act 1978. Class 3B insurers must:

- Maintain a solvency margin and an enhanced capital requirement, with capital adequacy reported via the Bermuda Solvency Capital Requirement model (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- Maintain a minimum liquidity ratio of at least 75% of relevant liabilities (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- Obtain BMA prior approval for any reduction of total statutory capital by 15% or more, or by 25% or more of total statutory capital and surplus (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- File annual GAAP and statutory financial statements within four months of year-end; statutory financials are not part of the BMA's public records, but GAAP financials are publicly available (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

The **Bermuda Corporate Income Tax Act of 2023** ("CIT") was effective January 1, 2025 and imposes a 15% corporate income tax on in-scope entities (Bermuda-resident or with Bermuda permanent establishment), regardless of prior assurances under the Exempted Undertakings Tax Protection Act 1966 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). The **Bermuda Tax Credits Act 2025** was enacted in December 2025, introducing qualified refundable tax credits ("QRTCs") for entities with on-island operational presence — Arch recognized the full-year effect of the 2025 QRTCs in Q4 2025, with the benefit flowing primarily through its Reinsurance segment expense ratio and corporate expenses (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The OECD's **Pillar II framework** imposing a 15% global minimum tax has been adopted in some jurisdictions (Ireland, EU member states) but not the U.S.; for Essent, the Bermuda CIT is in effect a "de-facto" Pillar II adoption (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Three reasons the regulatory landscape is operationally consequential.

First, **the constraint stack is tight and active**. Each MI subsidiary has to remain in simultaneous compliance with state RTC limits, PMIERs Available/Required assets, GSE-letter conditions on reinsurance credit (the 18:1 RTC ceiling), Bermuda Class 3B solvency margins (where applicable), and the dividend-capacity rules of each domicile. The PMIERs framework alone has been amended multiple times since adoption in 2015 and the most recent August 2024 update is still phasing in through September 2026 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31). The Bermuda CIT and Tax Credits Act 2025 changes during 2024-2025 introduced new variables on the after-tax line for the four MIs with Bermuda exposure.

Second, **dividend capacity is a chokepoint**. Because all six MIs operate as holding companies dependent on their insurance subsidiaries' upstream dividends to fund debt service, share repurchases, and dividends to public shareholders, state regulator approvals on extraordinary dividends are a practical constraint on capital return. In 2025, MGIC paid $800 million in dividends to its holding company (a 7% increase from $750 million in 2024); MGIC can pay $89 million of ordinary dividends in 2026 without OCI approval (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Essent Guaranty paid $495 million to Essent US Holdings in 2025 and has $246 million of ordinary dividend capacity for 2026 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Radian Guaranty distributed $795 million to Radian Group in 2025; in connection with the Inigo acquisition funding via a $600 million intercompany note, Radian Guaranty's dividends will require Pennsylvania Insurance Department approval for at least three years (and possibly up to five) (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Third, **regulatory change is a tailwind or headwind on a 12-24 month lag**. The August 2024 PMIERs Update phase-in (March 31, 2025 to September 30, 2026) is the most active near-term regulatory event. The NAIC Model Act revised in August 2023 has not yet been adopted by any state — but Wisconsin has begun the process and could move first. Federal-level changes (FHA premium actions, Basel III Endgame implications for bank capital relief on PMI) remain on the watchlist (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

- **State regulators by domicile**: Wisconsin OCI (MGIC, NMIC, Re One); Pennsylvania Insurance Department (Radian Guaranty, Essent Guaranty); North Carolina Department of Insurance (Enact's EMICO).
- **State RTC ratio limit**: most states cap at 25:1; Wisconsin's formula-based limit is slightly higher (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31); GSE letters cap at 18:1 for purposes of PMIERs reinsurance credit (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Statutory contingency reserve at the largest MIs**: Enact $4,513M (YE2025); Essent Guaranty $2,600M (YE2025); MGIC, Radian Guaranty, NMIC, and Arch each maintain their own contingency reserves under similar 50%-of-NEP rules (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **2026 ordinary dividend capacity**: MGIC $89M (Wisconsin OCI) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31); Essent Guaranty $246M (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31); Enact's insurance subsidiaries $3M (NCDOI) (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31); Essent Re $423M as of January 1, 2026 (BMA) (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **PMIERs status**: All six MIs reported PMIERs compliance at year-end 2025; the August 2024 PMIERs Update was 50% phased in (March 31 and September 30, 2025) with 75% (March 31, 2026) and 100% (September 30, 2026) still to come (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Bermuda regulatory state**: Bermuda CIT effective January 1, 2025 (15%); Bermuda Tax Credits Act 2025 enacted December 2025; Essent Re, Enact Re, and Arch's Bermuda operations subject to Class 3B BMA oversight (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **NAIC Model Act adoption**: not yet formally adopted by any state; Wisconsin process underway with modifications expected (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Federal**: HOPA (1998) governs cancellation; Dodd-Frank QM/QRM rules govern lender retention obligations; CFPB regulation continues; FHA premium environment stable (no further FHA premium reductions in 2025) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

**1998**: Homeowners Protection Act ("HOPA") establishes federal cancellation framework for borrower-paid MI (78% LTV automatic; 80% LTV on borrower request).

**2008**: GSEs placed in FHFA conservatorship; significant subsequent regulatory recalibration.

**2010**: Dodd-Frank Act passed; QRM Rule and QM framework introduce risk-retention requirements.

**December 31, 2015**: Original PMIERs effective — first GSE-coordinated codified eligibility framework for MIs (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**June 30, 2020**: PMIERs amendment introduces the 0.30 multiplier on the risk-based required asset factor for non-performing loans backed by properties in FEMA Declared Major Disaster Areas (eligibility-extended for COVID-era forbearance), an accommodation later discontinued effective March 31, 2025 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

**2019**: Most recent large-scale revisions to PMIERs; subsequent updates address specific matters (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

**February 2023**: FHA reduces annual MI premium by 30 basis points for most new borrowers — affecting PMI competitive economics (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**August 2023**: NAIC adopts revised Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Model Act, covering capital, contingency reserves, investments, underwriting, quality control, and reinsurance — adoption by states pending (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**August 21, 2024**: GSEs and FHFA release the PMIERs Update (asset-class-and-credit-rating-based refinements to Available Assets) phased in March 31, 2025 to September 30, 2026 (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

**January 1, 2025**: Bermuda Corporate Income Tax (CIT) effective, imposing a 15% corporate income tax on in-scope Bermuda-resident entities (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Essent Title Insurance, Inc. renamed (effective January 1, 2025) from Agents National Title Insurance Company (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

**March 31, 2025**: 0.30x PMIERs multiplier on COVID-era forbearance loans is discontinued — contributing to a step-up in PMIERs Required Assets at MIs that were carrying COVID-era non-performing loans (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

**December 2025**: Bermuda enacts the Tax Credits Act 2025, introducing qualified refundable tax credits ("QRTCs"); Arch recognizes the full-year 2025 effect in Q4 2025, materially affecting the Reinsurance segment expense ratio and corporate expense run-rate (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**Forward**: The August 2024 PMIERs Update continues phasing in (75% at March 31, 2026; 100% at September 30, 2026); state-level adoption of the revised NAIC Model Act remains uncertain; potential GSE conservatorship-release activity could reshape PMIERs and the broader regulatory framework (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
