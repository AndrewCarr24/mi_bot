# NMI Holdings, Inc. (NMIH)

> NMI Holdings, Inc. is the publicly traded Delaware holding company for National Mortgage Insurance Corporation ("NMIC"), a Wisconsin-domiciled GSE-approved monoline mortgage insurer that operates as "National MI." NMIH was incorporated in May 2011, began operations in 2012, and wrote its first MI policy in 2013. At year-end 2025 NMI Holdings held a record $221.4 billion of primary IIF and $59.3 billion of primary RIF on $48.9 billion of 2025 NIW; the PMIERs sufficiency ratio was 70% with $3.5 billion of Available Assets, $2.1 billion of risk-based required assets, and $1.4 billion of excess; and the company earned $388.9 million of net income (up 8% from 2024) and $4.92 per diluted share (up 11%) on a 16.2% return on equity. Book value per share excluding accumulated unrealized gains/losses was $34.58 at year-end 2025, up 16% year-over-year (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## What it is

NMI Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: NMIH) is a Delaware corporation that was incorporated in May 2011, began start-up operations in 2012, and wrote its first mortgage insurance policy in 2013 (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31). The company operates under the "National MI" brand name. As of year-end 2025 the company had 225 full-time and part-time employees and operated through three wholly owned subsidiaries:

- **National Mortgage Insurance Corporation ("NMIC")** — the primary insurance subsidiary, a Wisconsin-domiciled mortgage insurer principally regulated by the Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance, approved as an MI provider by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, and licensed to write MI coverage in all 50 states and the District of Columbia (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **National Mortgage Reinsurance Inc. One ("Re One")** — a Wisconsin-domiciled wholly owned subsidiary that historically provided reinsurance coverage to NMIC under certain statutory risk-retention requirements that have since been repealed; the reinsurance coverage from Re One to NMIC has been commuted, and Re One does not currently have active insurance exposures (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **National Mortgage Insurance Services, Inc. ("NMIS")** — provides outsourced loan-review services to mortgage lenders (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31).

NMIH provides mortgage insurance on residential first-lien mortgage loans through NMIC. As of year-end 2025 the company had issued master policies to 2,193 customers — including national and regional mortgage banks, money center banks, credit unions, community banks, builder-owned mortgage lenders, internet-sourced lenders, and other non-bank lenders — with over 1,700 active accounts and 90 new lenders activated during 2025 (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The company has approximately 680,000 policies in force at year-end 2025 (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Pricing of new business is delivered through NMI's proprietary "Rate GPS" pricing engine, which the CEO describes as enabling granular mix management across approximately 950 geographic units in the U.S. mortgage market (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

NMI is a post-2011 entrant to U.S. private mortgage insurance — a 2012 start-up rather than a legacy or carve-out franchise — and that origin shapes three features that distinguish the company.

First, **NMI's book was built entirely after 2013**. The company began writing MI policies only in 2013, so essentially all of its in-force policies were originated under post-GFC underwriting standards and do not carry any pre-2009 legacy exposure. That dynamic, combined with active risk-mix management through Rate GPS, was associated with a 1.12% default rate at year-end 2025 (7,661 defaults across the portfolio); management characterizes its credit performance as "industry-leading" (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **the reinsurance program is structured as a forward-flow standing program with multi-year coverage**. In Q4 2025 NMI entered into a series of new quota share and excess-of-loss treaties that, together with the prior layered program, provide forward-flow coverage for all new business produced through 2028 at an estimated 4% pretax cost of capital; management characterized these deals as "amongst the best we've ever achieved in terms of their cost, capacity, duration, and structure" (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Reinsurance is described as "a deep, secure, and efficient source of PMIERs growth capital" rather than an opportunistic risk-distribution lever (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **NMI is approaching parity NIW share among the six U.S. mortgage insurers**. Industry NIW for 2025 was approximately $310 billion (per CEO Adam Pollitzer); NMI's $48.9 billion of 2025 NIW implies roughly 15.8% market share. On the Q4 2025 call an analyst (KBW's Rick Shane) characterized NMI as "very, very close to parity market share" and asked whether 2026 would be the year of full parity; management's response did not target a parity figure but rather emphasized that NMI does not target market share, instead pointing to on-the-ground execution, customer activations, and mix management as the drivers of recent share gains. At parity, each of the six insurers would carry approximately 16.7% (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

The principal external risk factor that management focused on in the Q4 2025 call is potential FHA premium reductions: management's framing is that the FHA's capital position has structural challenges (capital, regulatory, budget) and that further FHA rate adjustments would impose additional risk on taxpayers given that the private MI industry is "ready, willing, and able" to provide the necessary support (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

NMI Holdings was incorporated in May 2011, began operations in 2012, and wrote its first MI policy in 2013 (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31). That history has three implications for how the company has evolved through 2025.

First, **the in-force book has been built entirely under post-2013 underwriting standards**. Customer count has grown steadily from a standing start to 2,193 master-policy holders at year-end 2025; active accounts exceeded 1,700 with 90 new lenders activated during 2025 alone (NMIH_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The result by 2025 is a $221.4 billion primary IIF book — up 5.4% year-over-year — with a 1.12% default rate that management characterizes as "industry-leading credit performance" (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **financial scale has compounded with the book**. Net income rose 8% year-over-year to $388.9 million in 2025, diluted EPS rose 11% to $4.92, and return on equity reached 16.2% — all records (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Book value per share excluding net unrealized gains/losses grew 16% over 2025 to $34.58 (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **the reinsurance program has been built layer-by-layer to provide standing forward-flow coverage**. The Q4 2025 transactions extended forward-flow coverage to NIW originated through 2028 at an estimated 4% pretax cost of capital, characterized by management as among the company's best-ever in cost, capacity, duration, and structure (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The program is structured as both a tail-risk hedge and as a standing source of PMIERs growth capital.

Capital return has accelerated alongside earnings: since the buyback program's inception in 2022, the company has repurchased $349 million of common stock (12.1 million shares) at an average price of $28.89; Q4 2025 alone saw $31 million of repurchases at an average price of $37.72, and $226 million of repurchase capacity remains under the existing authorization (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

The 2025 trajectory in persistency reflects the start of a refinance-related normalization. 12-month persistency declined from 83.9% in Q3 2025 to 83.4% in Q4 2025 as mortgage rates rallied during the quarter and stimulated both refinance and purchase activity. Management has framed this as a beginning of natural turnover in the more recent (2023-2025) vintages with lower embedded equity, while the older 2020-2022 vintages remain "sticky" given record-low note rates (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
