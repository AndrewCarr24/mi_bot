# Essent Group Ltd. (ESNT)

> Essent Group Ltd. is a Bermuda-domiciled holding company that writes private mortgage insurance in the U.S. through Pennsylvania-domiciled Essent Guaranty, reinsures U.S. mortgage and GSE credit-risk-transfer business through Bermuda Class 3B reinsurer Essent Re, and operates a small title insurance and settlement business acquired in July 2023. At year-end 2025 Essent held $248.4 billion of primary mortgage insurance in force, posted a 169% PMIERs sufficiency ratio with $1.4 billion of excess available assets, and earned $690 million of net income ($6.90 per diluted share) on a 12% return on average equity. The two strategic developments of 2025 were the fourth-quarter recasting of segment reporting — splitting third-party reinsurance out from U.S. mortgage insurance into its own reportable segment to reflect Essent Re's expansion — and Essent Re's entry into the Lloyd's of London market via funds-at-Lloyd's to write property-and-casualty reinsurance starting in 2026 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## What it is

Essent Group Ltd. (NYSE: ESNT) was organized as a Bermuda exempted limited liability company on July 1, 2008 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). It serves the U.S. housing finance industry through three operating subsidiaries:

- **Essent Guaranty, Inc.** — a Pennsylvania-domiciled monoline mortgage guaranty insurer licensed in all 50 states and the District of Columbia, approved by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, and the principal U.S. operating company. Essent Guaranty writes private mortgage insurance on residential first-lien loans with down payments of less than 20% and also offers contract underwriting services (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Essent Reinsurance Ltd. ("Essent Re")** — a Bermuda-based Class 3B insurer regulated by the Bermuda Monetary Authority. Essent Re reinsures Essent Guaranty's NIW under an intercompany quota share, participates in Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac credit-risk-transfer transactions, provides underwriting consulting to third-party reinsurers, has reinsured mortgage risk in Australia and France, and entered the Lloyd's of London market in the fourth quarter of 2025 to write property-and-casualty reinsurance starting in 2026 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Essent Title Insurance, Inc.** (renamed effective January 1, 2025 from Agents National Title Insurance Company) and **Boston National Title** — a Pennsylvania-domiciled title insurance underwriter and a national title agency, both acquired on July 1, 2023. The title operations are reported within "Corporate and Other" (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

In the fourth quarter of 2025, Essent recast its segment reporting in connection with Essent Re's expansion into Lloyd's-related P&C lines: the previously aggregated U.S. mortgage insurance and GSE/other risk-share businesses were separated into two reportable segments — **Mortgage Insurance** and **Reinsurance** — with all prior-period information conformed to the new presentation (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Customers select a coverage percentage on each insured loan, generally between 6% and 35%, with Essent's risk averaging approximately 27% of the underlying primary IIF (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). The Reinsurance segment, primarily Essent Re's GSE risk-share book, covered approximately $2.3 billion of risk at year-end 2025 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Essent occupies an unusual structural position among the six U.S. private mortgage insurers. Three features stand out.

First, Essent operates an explicit "**buy, manage and distribute**" model: at year-end 2025 approximately 98% of the mortgage insurance portfolio was subject to some form of reinsurance — fully collateralized excess-of-loss with insurance-linked-note special-purpose insurers, traditional XOL with third-party reinsurers, and quota share programs with both Essent Re (intercompany) and panels of third-party reinsurers (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). The intercompany quota share with Essent Re stepped up from 25% (pre-2021) to 35% (2021-2024) to **50% effective January 1, 2025** (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Second, Bermuda domicile materially shapes the after-tax economics. Essent Group and Essent Re are domiciled in Bermuda and the company's 2025 consolidated effective tax rate was 16% (with $2.1 million of favorable discrete items); management guided to approximately 17% for 2026 excluding discrete items (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The 10-K's Legislative and Regulatory Developments section addresses the Bermuda Corporate Income Tax separately (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Third, Essent Re is sized to be more than a passive captive. At year-end 2025 it had **$1.7 billion of total equity**, generated approximately $80 million in third-party net income for the year, and ended with $2.3 billion of risk in force; it carries an A- (stable) rating from S&P and an A (Excellent, stable) rating from A.M. Best (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). The fourth-quarter 2025 Lloyd's expansion — backed by a $50 million Funds-at-Lloyd's contribution that sits on Essent Re's existing balance sheet rather than requiring fresh capital — is described by management as a measured extension of an existing platform rather than a new line of business, with $100-$150 million of written premium expected in 2026 (about two-thirds earned in 2026), at a combined ratio "consistent with a diversified P&C reinsurance company" (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Management has also emphasized that Essent does not optimize for market share. CEO Mark Casale described Essent as "kind of the 15, 16" share player and explicitly stated that the company would rather return a marginal dollar to shareholders than chase low-priced new business (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). That posture is reflected in the share-count trajectory: during 2025 the company repurchased nearly 10% of the shares outstanding at year-end 2024 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

Essent Group Ltd. was organized as a Bermuda exempted limited liability company on July 1, 2008 (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Three threads have shaped the company's trajectory.

First, the **reinsurance posture has steadily intensified**. The intercompany quota share between Essent Guaranty and Essent Re stepped from 25% (pre-2021) to 35% (2021-2024) to 50% (January 2025 onward), and at year-end 2025 approximately 98% of the mortgage insurance portfolio was covered by some form of third-party or intercompany reinsurance (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The third-party quota share program has been written on a forward basis: separate transactions cover 25% of 2025 NIW, 25% of 2026 NIW, and 20% of 2027 NIW (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

Second, the **capital structure was simplified and termed out** in 2024. On July 1, 2024 Essent issued $500 million of 6.25% Senior Notes due 2029 in an underwritten public offering and used $425 million of the proceeds to repay the term loan portion of its existing secured credit facility; concurrently, the Fourth Amended and Restated Credit Agreement increased revolving capacity from $400 million to $500 million (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). At year-end 2025 the revolver remained undrawn and the debt-to-capital ratio was 8% (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **Essent Re has been expanded into a multi-line reinsurance platform**. The acquisition of Agents National Title Insurance Company (renamed Essent Title effective January 1, 2025) and Boston National Title on July 1, 2023 added title insurance and settlement; management characterizes the title business as an incubation-stage, primarily centralized refinance platform that is unlikely to contribute materially to earnings absent a meaningful decline in mortgage rates (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). More consequentially, in Q4 2025 Essent Re entered the Lloyd's of London market via Funds-at-Lloyd's to begin writing P&C reinsurance in 2026; as part of the same evolution, the company recast its segment reporting in Q4 2025 to separate Reinsurance from U.S. Mortgage Insurance, all prior periods conformed (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31).

The headline financial trajectory through 2025 reflects these structural shifts plus a normalizing credit cycle. NIW eased modestly from $47.7 billion in 2023 to $45.6 billion in 2024 before recovering to $46.6 billion in 2025; IIF grew at a roughly 2% pace; the average net premium rate has been stable at 35 basis points across 2023, 2024, and 2025; defaults rose from 14,819 at year-end 2023 to 18,439 at year-end 2024 (with hurricanes Helene and Milton accounting for approximately 2,119 of the 2024 increase) to 20,210 at year-end 2025, lifting the default rate to 2.5% (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). Capital return has accelerated alongside, culminating in a ~10% reduction in share count during 2025 and a 13% dividend increase to $0.35 per share for 2026 (ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
