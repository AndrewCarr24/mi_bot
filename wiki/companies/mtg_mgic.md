# MGIC Investment Corporation (MTG)

> MGIC Investment Corporation is the Wisconsin-organized holding company for Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation. In 2025 the company crossed the $300 billion insurance-in-force threshold for the first time, ending the year with $303.1 billion of direct primary IIF, $81.2 billion of RIF, and $60.2 billion of NIW; MGIC's share of PMI industry NIW was 19.4% per *Inside Mortgage Finance*, up from 18.6% in 2024. MGIC earned $738 million of net income ($3.14 per diluted share) on a 14.3% return on equity; PMIERs Available Assets totaled $5.7 billion against $3.2 billion of Minimum Required Assets ($2.5 billion cushion); and the company returned approximately $915 million to shareholders — a 124% payout ratio — while reducing the share count by 12%. Moody's upgraded MGIC's financial strength rating from A3 to A2 during 2025 and S&P revised the outlook on MGIC and its core operating subsidiaries to positive (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## What it is

MGIC Investment Corporation (NYSE: MTG) is a Wisconsin corporation organized in 1984 with its principal office in Milwaukee (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). It operates as a holding company through wholly owned subsidiaries that provide private mortgage insurance, other mortgage credit risk management solutions, and ancillary services.

The principal operating subsidiary is **Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation ("MGIC")** — a Wisconsin-domiciled mortgage insurer regulated by the Office of the Commissioner of Insurance of the State of Wisconsin (OCI), licensed in all 50 states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, and Guam, and approved by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). MGIC writes primary mortgage insurance on residential first-lien low-down-payment loans (loans with less than 20% down payments).

**MGIC Assurance Corporation ("MAC")** is a separately identified subsidiary of MGIC; in connection with the GSEs' credit risk transfer programs, an insurance subsidiary of MGIC provides insurance and reinsurance covering portions of credit risk on certain GSE reference pools — approximately $482 million of risk at year-end 2025, up from $392 million at year-end 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Pricing of new business is executed through risk-based pricing systems and customized rate plans rather than standard rate cards (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Customer concentration eased in 2025: the top ten customers accounted for approximately 32% of NIW (down from 37% in 2024), and the largest single customer accounted for approximately 16% of 2025 NIW (down from 21% in 2024) and approximately 11% of 2025 direct earned premiums (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

MGIC's share of PMI industry NIW was 19.4% in 2025, up from 18.6% in 2024, per *Inside Mortgage Finance* (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Total private mortgage insurance industry NIW was $311 billion in 2025, $299 billion in 2024, and $284 billion in 2023 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Three features make MGIC particularly relevant for analysts.

First, **MGIC is the bellwether for industry credit normalization**. Through 2025 the delinquency rate edged up to 2.43% (from 2.40% at year-end 2024 and 2.25% at year-end 2023) as the post-COVID delinquency inventory has largely returned to pre-pandemic levels (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). The pace of new notices and the favorable reserve development have both moderated from earlier post-COVID years; Q4 2025 favorable development was $31 million versus more substantial releases in earlier quarters of 2025, with the initial claim rate assumption on new notices held at 7.5% (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **the reinsurance program has materially restructured how MGIC manages capital**. As of year-end 2025, approximately 73.8% of IIF was subject to QSR transactions (up from 68.2% at year-end 2024), and approximately 87.2% of 2025 NIW was subject to QSR transactions (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Total reinsurance arrangements reduced PMIERs required assets by approximately $2.8 billion, or roughly 47%, at year-end 2025 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). During Q4 2025 alone, MGIC executed a $250 million excess-of-loss transaction covering 2021 NIW, a 40% quota share covering most of 2027 NIW, and a renegotiation of the 2022-NIW QSR treaties that is expected to reduce that treaty's cost by approximately 40% beginning in 2026 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). In January 2026 MGIC completed an eighth insurance-linked-note transaction providing $324 million of additional loss protection on policies written between January 2022 and March 2025 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **MGIC has become a high-payout-ratio capital-return story**. Across 2025 the company returned approximately $915 million to shareholders through dividends and repurchases, equating to a 124% payout ratio of the year's net income; the share count was reduced by 12% over the year, the per-share quarterly dividend was raised in Q3 2025 (the fifth consecutive annual increase), and management characterizes the firm as prioritizing prudent IIF growth alongside capital return rather than maximizing one over the other (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The company's exposure to FHA pricing dynamics is the principal external swing factor: the FHA accounted for an estimated 34.3% of the low-down-payment market in 2025 (versus 33.5% in 2024 and 33.2% in 2023) and the VA for 26.8% (versus 24.5% and 21.5%), so further FHA pricing actions or VA program expansion would directly affect MGIC's growth runway (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

- **Total revenues**: $1.2 billion
- **Net income**: $738 million ($3.14 per diluted share); fourth-quarter net income was $169 million ($0.75 per diluted share)
- **Return on equity**: 14.3% for the full year (13% annualized in Q4)
- **Book value per share**: $23.47 at December 31, 2025, up 13% year-over-year
- **Shares outstanding**: 214,953,360 as of February 20, 2026
- **In-force premium yield**: 38 basis points in Q4 2025 (relatively stable through 2025; management guides to "near 38 basis points" again in 2026)
- **Investment portfolio book yield**: 4.0% at year-end 2025 (the pre-tax yield was also 4.0% for 2025 vs. 4.0% in 2024 and 3.7% in 2023); fourth-quarter investment income was $62 million
- **Underwriting and other expenses**: $201 million for 2025 (down $17 million from 2024); guided to $190-$200 million for 2026, with the decline driven primarily by higher expected ceding commissions on renegotiated quota share treaties

Portfolio:
- **Direct primary IIF**: $303.1 billion at December 31, 2025, up approximately 3% year-over-year — and the first time any U.S. mortgage insurer surpassed $300 billion of IIF (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Direct primary RIF**: $81.2 billion
- **NIW**: $60.2 billion in 2025 (versus $55.7 billion in 2024 and $46.1 billion in 2023)
- **Persistency**: annual persistency was 85% at year-end 2025 (held elevated and stable throughout 2025 per management) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Average credit score at origination**: 748
- **Delinquency rate**: 2.43% at year-end 2025 (vs. 2.40% at year-end 2024 and 2.25% at year-end 2023)

Capital and PMIERs:
- **PMIERs**: MGIC's Available Assets totaled $5.7 billion at December 31, 2025, $2.5 billion in excess of its Minimum Required Assets of approximately $3.2 billion (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Reinsurance PMIERs credit**: total reinsurance arrangements (QSR + XOL + ILN) reduced PMIERs required assets by approximately $2.8 billion, or roughly 47%, at year-end 2025 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Holding-company liquidity**: $1.0 billion of cash and investments at the holding company at year-end 2025 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Subsidiary dividend flow**: MGIC paid $800 million in dividends to the holding company in 2025 (a 7% increase from $750 million in 2024); in 2026 MGIC can pay $89 million of ordinary dividends without OCI approval, before considering dividends paid in the preceding twelve months (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31)

Reinsurance program (selected actions during 2025 and early 2026):
- $250 million excess-of-loss transaction covering 2021 NIW (Q4 2025) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- 40% quota share covering most of 2027 NIW (Q4 2025) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- Renegotiation of 2022-NIW QSR treaties to reduce ongoing cost by approximately 40% starting in 2026 (Q4 2025) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- Eighth insurance-linked-note transaction providing $324 million of loss protection on policies written between January 2022 and March 2025 (January 2026) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)

Capital return in 2025:
- **Total return to shareholders**: approximately $915 million (124% payout ratio of FY 2025 net income), comprising share repurchases and dividends (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Share count reduction**: 12% over the year (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Quarterly dividend**: $0.56 per share for full year 2025 (a 14% increase from $0.49 per share in 2024); the quarterly dividend was raised by 15% in Q3 2025, marking the fifth consecutive year of dividend growth (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Q4 2025 specifics**: $33 million paid in dividends plus 6.8 million shares repurchased for $189 million (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **January 2026**: an additional 2.7 million shares were repurchased for $73 million; the board approved a $0.15 per-share quarterly dividend payable on March 6, 2026 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)

Ratings: during 2025, **Moody's upgraded MGIC's financial strength rating from A3 to A2**, and **S&P revised its outlook on MGIC Investment Corporation and its core operating subsidiaries (including MGIC) from stable to positive** (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Debt: long-term debt obligations are owed by the holding company, principally the 5.25% Senior Notes due on August 15, 2028 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

MGIC traces its franchise to the origin of modern private mortgage insurance: management characterizes the company as "the founder of modern private mortgage insurance nearly seventy years ago" (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). MGIC Investment Corporation, the publicly traded Wisconsin holding company, was organized in 1984 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Three threads define the trajectory through 2025.

First, **NIW expansion alongside disciplined market posture**. NIW grew from $46.1 billion in 2023 to $55.7 billion in 2024 to $60.2 billion in 2025, reflecting both market growth and a higher targeted MGIC market position (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Market share rose from approximately 18.6% in 2024 to 19.4% in 2025, while customer concentration eased meaningfully (top-10 share fell from 37% to 32%, largest customer fell from 21% to 16% of NIW) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Second, **the reinsurance program has been progressively built out as a standing capital-management lever**. Coverage of in-force IIF under QSR transactions rose from 68.2% at year-end 2024 to 73.8% at year-end 2025; coverage of new NIW remained at approximately 87% (87.2% in 2025, 86.9% in 2024) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). On top of QSR, the company has executed a series of eight insurance-linked-note transactions through January 2026 with the Home Re Entities (Bermuda special-purpose insurers) and traditional XOL transactions, plus a deliberate strategy of recapturing risk on attractive seasoned vintages while reinsuring forward NIW; the 2022-NIW QSR renegotiation in Q4 2025 was an explicit application of that strategy (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The cumulative effect is that approximately 47% of PMIERs required assets is offset by reinsurance credit (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **the capital-return cadence has accelerated**. In 2025 MGIC returned approximately $915 million to shareholders, equating to a 124% payout ratio; the dividend per share rose from $0.49 in 2024 to $0.56 in 2025 (a 14% increase, with the Q3 2025 raise marking the fifth consecutive annual increase); and the share count was reduced by 12% during the year (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). MGIC paid $800 million in dividends to the holding company in 2025, a 7% increase from $750 million in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). At the same time, the rating-agency posture improved during 2025 with Moody's lifting the financial strength rating to A2 and S&P revising the outlook to positive (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

The headline credit signal in 2025 was modest normalization rather than deterioration: the delinquency rate ticked up to 2.43% from 2.40% at year-end 2024, but the year-over-year increase was the slowest since 2024, and management has framed this as the continuation of credit conditions returning to pre-COVID norms rather than an inflection in either direction (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
