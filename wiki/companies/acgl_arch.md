# Arch Capital Group Ltd. (ACGL)

> Arch Capital Group Ltd. is a publicly listed Bermuda-domiciled diversified specialty insurer and reinsurer that is part of the S&P 500 and operates three reportable segments — Insurance, Reinsurance, and Mortgage — through wholly owned subsidiaries on a worldwide basis. For 2025, Arch wrote $16.5 billion of net premiums, generated $3.7 billion of after-tax operating income (a record), reported $4.4 billion of net income available to common shareholders, and ended the year with approximately $26.9 billion of capital and $47.4 billion of investable assets. Book value per share grew 22.6% to $65.11. The Mortgage segment — Arch's U.S. private mortgage insurance business plus international MI and GSE credit-risk-share — produced $1.0 billion of underwriting income for the year, the fourth consecutive year above the $1.0 billion threshold; in U.S. primary mortgage insurance, persistency was 81.8% in Q4 2025 and the delinquency rate was 2.17% (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## What it is

Arch Capital Group Ltd. (NASDAQ: ACGL) is a publicly listed Bermuda exempted company headquartered at 100 Pitts Bay Road, Pembroke HM 08, Bermuda (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). Founded in 2001, the company was celebrating its 25th anniversary at the end of 2025 (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). It operates three reportable segments through wholly owned subsidiaries:

- **Insurance segment** — specialty property, casualty, and other lines written across North America (including alternative markets, construction, and E&S casualty), Bermuda, Continental Europe, and London (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **Reinsurance segment** — property catastrophe, property other than catastrophe, casualty, and specialty reinsurance, written globally including through London-market platforms acquired with Barbican in 2019 and Continental European operations (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **Mortgage segment** — U.S. and international mortgage insurance plus participation in GSE credit-risk-sharing transactions. The U.S. mortgage platform was established in 2014 and expanded materially through the 2016 acquisition of United Guaranty Corporation; international operations include Arch Insurance (EU) (Ireland-based, authorized in 2011 for European/U.K. mortgage insurance) and the Australian lenders mortgage insurance business written principally through Arch Lenders Mortgage Indemnity Ltd. ("Arch Indemnity"), the former Westpac Lenders Mortgage Insurance Limited acquired in August 2021 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

The U.S. mortgage operations include providers approved as eligible mortgage insurers by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac under the PMIERs and write primary mortgage insurance on residential first-lien loans (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

Arch also has meaningful equity-method investments (notably Somers Re/REIT) that contribute outside the underwriting segments, and at year-end 2025 held an investment portfolio of $47.4 billion (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Among the six U.S. private mortgage insurers, Arch is distinctive in that its mortgage insurance business sits within a globally diversified P&C and reinsurance group rather than as the franchise itself. Three implications follow for analysts.

First, **U.S. mortgage insurance is one of three large profit pools, not the franchise**. The 2025 Mortgage segment delivered $1.0 billion of underwriting income (the fourth consecutive year exceeding $1.0 billion) — but the Reinsurance segment contributed a record $1.6 billion of underwriting income on the year, and the Insurance segment contributed $119 million in Q4 2025 alone (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Capital allocation is therefore explicitly across-segment: management has emphasized cycle management as a foundational principle, including pivoting the Insurance group's underwriters to the most attractive specialty lines and adjusting Reinsurance writings as January 1, 2026 property-cat and short-tail XOL renewals saw 10-20% rate decreases (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **the diversified structure provides cycle-management flexibility unavailable to monoline MIs**. Across Q4 2025, Arch reported a consolidated combined ratio of 80.6% with $118 million of pretax favorable prior-year development (2.8 points on the combined ratio), recognized across all three segments — including the Mortgage segment, which benefited from strong cure activity on delinquent mortgages, and short-tail P&C lines with seasoned reserves (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The current accident year combined ratio for the Mortgage segment was 34% in Q4 2025 (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **Bermuda domicile and the 2025 Bermuda Tax Credits Act materially shape after-tax economics**. In December 2025 Bermuda enacted the Tax Credits Act 2025, which introduces qualified refundable tax credits (QRTCs) for in-scope entities with on-island operational presence. Arch recognized the full-year effect of the 2025 QRTCs in Q4 2025, which flows primarily through the Reinsurance segment expense ratio and corporate expenses; Arch's effective tax rate for FY 2025 was 14.9% (vs. its previously guided 16-18% range, with 1.4 points of benefit from discrete items), and management guided to a 16-18% ETR for 2026 (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). For 2026 specifically, the QRTCs are expected to push the Reinsurance segment operating expense ratio to a 3.9-4.5% range and reduce corporate expenses to roughly $80-90 million (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

For mortgage-insurance-focused analysts, the most relevant headline is that Arch's U.S. primary mortgage insurance business is being managed through a constrained-origination market: NIW remains modest, IIF is roughly flat year-over-year, and persistency was 81.8% in Q4 2025 (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

Group-level results for FY 2025 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31):

- **Capital**: approximately $26.9 billion at December 31, 2025
- **Net premiums written**: $16.5 billion
- **Net income available to Arch common shareholders**: $4.4 billion
- **After-tax operating income**: $3.7 billion (a record); $1.1 billion in Q4 2025 (up 26% YoY)
- **After-tax operating earnings per share**: $9.84 for FY 2025; $2.98 per share in Q4 2025
- **Operating return on average common equity**: 17.1% annualized for the full year; Q4 2025 net income ROE was 21.2% annualized
- **Book value per share**: $65.11 at December 31, 2025, up 22.6% year-over-year (from $53.11 at year-end 2024); BVPS grew 4.5% in Q4 2025 alone
- **Investable assets**: $47.4 billion at year-end 2025; Q4 2025 net investment income was $434 million plus $155 million from equity-method investments (combined $589 million, or $1.60 per share pretax)
- **Operating cash flow**: $6.2 billion for the year
- **Effective tax rate (operating)**: 14.9% for 2025 (vs. 16-18% guided range), aided by 1.4 points of discrete-item benefit and the Bermuda QRTCs; 2026 guidance reverts to 16-18%

Segment underwriting income for 2025 (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31):
- **Insurance**: Q4 2025 underwriting income of $119 million; underlying ex-cat combined ratio of 90.8% (similar to Q4 2024); gross premiums written grew 2% year-over-year
- **Reinsurance**: $1.6 billion of underwriting income for the year (a record); Q4 2025 underwriting income of $458 million; Q4 2025 ex-cat ex-prior-year-development combined ratio of 74.9%; full-year 2025 combined ratio of 80.8% (lowest since 2016)
- **Mortgage**: $1.0 billion of underwriting income for the year (fourth consecutive year above $1.0 billion); Q4 2025 underwriting income of $250 million; Q4 2025 current accident year combined ratio of 34%

Mortgage segment specifics (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31):
- **USMI persistency**: 81.8% in Q4 2025 ("remaining high"), with USMI insurance in force relatively flat year-over-year
- **USMI delinquency rate**: 2.17% in Q4 2025 (consistent with management expectations)
- **NIW**: Q4 2025 USMI new insurance written was the highest of the year, but volumes remain modest given constrained origination conditions
- **Net premiums earned**: declined approximately $11 million sequentially within the segment, mostly across the CRT and Australian businesses
- **Reserve development**: favorable, driven by strong cure rates on delinquent mortgages

Cat exposure and capital management (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31):
- **Peak-zone net 1-in-250 PML**: $1.9 billion (flat year-over-year), or 8.2% of tangible shareholders' equity at January 1, 2026
- **2026 catastrophe-load estimate**: 7-8% of net earned premium (similar to the 2025 estimate)
- **Q4 2025 cat losses**: $164 million net of reinsurance and reinstatement premiums (lower than seasonally adjusted expectations but higher than the prior quarter), driven by U.S. severe convective storms, Hurricane Melissa, and a series of global events

Capital return:
- **2025 share repurchases**: $1.9 billion (21.2 million shares, equivalent to 5.6% of common shares outstanding at the start of 2025) (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **Q4 2025 share repurchases**: $798 million (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- **January 2026 through the earnings call**: an additional $349 million repurchased (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)
- The company has not set a payout target; capital return is a function of market opportunity, business growth, and stock price; management characterizes Arch as "well-positioned" for active share buybacks given current market conditions (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31)

The reinsurance market entering 2026 saw rate decreases of 10-20% on property catastrophe and other short-tail XOL renewals at January 1, with ceding commissions also rising in proportional reinsurance as supply outpaced demand (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

Arch was founded in 2001, and its 25-year compound annual growth rate in book value per share has exceeded 15% (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Over that span, the company has built each of its three segments through a combination of organic underwriting and selective acquisition.

The **Mortgage segment** — the franchise of interest for MI analysts — has the cleanest sequence of building blocks. The U.S. mortgage platform was established in 2014, then materially expanded through the 2016 acquisition of United Guaranty Corporation, which transformed Arch into one of the six U.S. private mortgage insurers (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). The European mortgage insurance business is written principally through Arch Insurance (EU), the Ireland-based carrier authorized in 2011 to provide European and U.K. mortgage insurance (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). The Australian platform came together in three steps: in 2019 Arch LMI Pty Ltd. was authorized by APRA to write lenders mortgage insurance directly; in August 2021 Arch acquired Westpac Lenders Mortgage Insurance Limited (renamed Arch Lenders Mortgage Indemnity Ltd., or "Arch Indemnity"); and in December 2022 Arch converted Arch LMI into a services company supporting the Australian LMI operations and the entity relinquished its APRA authorization. Arch Indemnity is the primary direct Australian LMI writer today (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31). On top of these primary platforms, Arch participates in GSE credit-risk-share transactions and writes mortgage reinsurance globally.

The **Reinsurance segment** has expanded through both organic growth and acquisitions, including a 2007 Danish underwriting agency focused on Accident & Health, the 2019 Barbican acquisition (London-market specialty reinsurance), and the 2021 acquisition of Somerset Bridge Group / Southern Rock Holdings (motor insurance MGA, distribution, and integrated claims) (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).

Operating performance through 2025 reflects three trends. First, the **Mortgage segment** has reached a steady-state level of $1.0 billion+ of annual underwriting income, marking four consecutive years above that threshold (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31); within USMI specifically, persistency was 81.8% in Q4 2025 and the delinquency rate has risen modestly to 2.17%, consistent with industry-wide normalization. Second, the **Reinsurance segment** posted its lowest combined ratio since 2016 (80.8% for FY 2025) and a record $1.6 billion of underwriting income, even as January 1, 2026 renewals came in with rate decreases of 10-20% on property catastrophe and short-tail XOL (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Third, **after-tax economics shifted in late 2025** with Bermuda's enactment of the Tax Credits Act 2025 in December: Arch recognized the full-year effect of the 2025 QRTCs in Q4, taking the FY 2025 effective tax rate to 14.9% (below the prior 16-18% guidance), and management guided 2026 ETR back to 16-18% with most of the QRTC benefit visible going forward in the Reinsurance segment expense ratio (3.9-4.5% guided) and corporate expenses ($80-90 million guided) (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Capital return has accelerated. Through 2025 Arch repurchased $1.9 billion of common stock (5.6% of starting shares outstanding), and management indicated active buyback intent into 2026 — $349 million had already been repurchased between year-end and the Q4 earnings call (ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Book value per share grew 22.6% during 2025, from $53.11 at year-end 2024 to $65.11 at year-end 2025 (ACGL_10-K_2025-12-31).
