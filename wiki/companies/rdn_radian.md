# Radian Group Inc. (RDN)

> Radian Group Inc. is the publicly traded insurance holding company whose principal operating subsidiary, Pennsylvania-domiciled Radian Guaranty Inc., is one of the six U.S. private mortgage insurers. Through 2025 the company underwent the largest strategic transformation in its history: on February 2, 2026 (after a September 2025 announcement) Radian closed the $1.67 billion all-cash acquisition of Inigo Limited — a Lloyd's specialty insurer that underwrites through Syndicate 1301 — and concurrently classified its Mortgage Conduit, Title, and Real Estate Services businesses as discontinued operations pending divestiture by Q3 2026. As a result, Radian's go-forward operating model is a two-line global specialty insurer (U.S. mortgage insurance + Lloyd's specialty insurance and reinsurance). The 2025 mortgage insurance business held a record $282.5 billion of primary IIF, $74.7 billion of RIF, and $55.2 billion of NIW; Radian Guaranty's PMIERs Cushion was $1.6 billion at year-end; and the company earned $618 million of net income from continuing operations ($4.39 per diluted share), returning $576 million to stockholders before pivoting holdco liquidity to fund the Inigo close (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## What it is

Radian Group Inc. (NYSE: RDN) is an insurance holding company. As of year-end 2025, its principal operating subsidiary is **Radian Guaranty Inc.**, a Pennsylvania-domiciled insurance company that is the GSE-approved insurer under the PMIERs, through which Radian provides private mortgage insurance on residential first-lien mortgages (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Three additional subsidiaries — **Radian Mortgage Capital LLC** (Delaware LLC, the Mortgage Conduit business), **Radian Title Insurance Inc.** (an Ohio-domiciled title insurance company), and Radian's Real Estate Services operations — were classified as held for sale in Q3 2025 following a comprehensive strategic review and have been reclassified to discontinued operations for all periods presented; management expects the divestiture to be completed no later than the end of Q3 2026 (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

In September 2025 Radian Group entered into a definitive agreement to acquire **Inigo Limited**, a U.K.-incorporated Lloyd's specialty insurer that underwrites through Syndicate 1301 (managed by Inigo Managing Agent Limited, with 97% of 2025 year-of-account capital provided by Inigo Corporate Member Limited). The transaction closed on **February 2, 2026** for $1.67 billion in cash, funded entirely from Radian Group's available liquidity (including a $600 million intercompany note from Radian Guaranty), with no new equity raised (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Inigo writes a diversified specialty book — property, casualty, financial lines, and other specialty insurance and reinsurance — with worldwide distribution through Lloyd's licenses (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Radian's segment structure was also recast during 2025. As of Q3 2025 the previously reported "All Other" category — which had captured holding-company income/losses, corporate expenses, the Mortgage Conduit, Title, and Real Estate Services businesses — was eliminated for continuing-operations reporting: discontinued operations are accounted for separately, and remaining holding-company items were reallocated into the Mortgage Insurance reportable segment for all prior and future periods (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Pricing of new business is delivered through Radian's proprietary RADAR Rates platform, which leverages the company's RADAR risk model; over 80% of 2025 NIW was sourced through RADAR Rates (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Radian executed the most ambitious strategic pivot of any U.S. private mortgage insurer in 2025-2026, redirecting from a single-line MI franchise toward a two-line global specialty insurance platform. Three implications follow.

First, **the Inigo acquisition fundamentally restructures the business model**. Management's framing is that the $1.67 billion purchase, financed from existing investment portfolio yielding 4-5% and redeployed into an operating business expected to earn mid-teens ROE through the cycle, generates approximately $170 million of incremental pretax net income — equivalent to roughly 200+ basis points of ROE accretion at a 25% U.K. statutory tax rate (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The transaction is expected to roughly double Radian's annual revenues and, in management's words, marks "a defining milestone in Radian's history" — the start of an evolution into a "global multi-line specialty insurer" (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **the funding mechanics matter for the mortgage insurance business**. The Inigo close was funded with a combination of Radian Group's existing liquidity, a $600 million intercompany note from Radian Guaranty (entered December 29, 2025; 10-year term; 6.50% interest; approved by the Pennsylvania Insurance Department), a $195 million Q4 2025 dividend from Radian Guaranty, and a $200 million January 2026 draw on the revolving credit facility (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The Intercompany Note reduced Radian Guaranty's PMIERs Cushion and the company's holding-company liquidity post-close was approximately $350 million; in addition, dividends from Radian Guaranty will require prior approval from the Pennsylvania Insurance Department for at least three years (and possibly up to five) in connection with the Inigo funding (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31). Management has guided to dividends of at least $600 million from Radian Guaranty in 2026 (including a $140 million Q1 2026 dividend), enabling repayment of the $200 million revolver draw while continuing to build holdco liquidity (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **the existing mortgage insurance franchise remains the cash-generation engine**. The 2025 MI portfolio reached an all-time high of $282.5 billion in primary IIF (up from $275.1 billion at year-end 2024) on $55.2 billion of NIW (vs. $52.0 billion in 2024), and Radian Guaranty distributed $795 million to Radian Group during 2025 through a combination of dividends and returns of capital (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The PMIERs Updates issued by the GSEs in August 2024 — phasing in revised Available-Assets standards at 25% (March 31, 2025), 50% (September 30, 2025), 75% (March 31, 2026), and 100% (September 30, 2026) — are not expected to have a material impact on Radian Guaranty's capital position or PMIERs Cushion (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Within the U.S. mortgage insurance market, Radian's NIW share was approximately 18% in 2025 (vs. 17% in 2024); the PMI industry's share of the total insured mortgage market was approximately 38% in 2025 (down from 41% in 2024), with FHA at approximately 35% (up from 34%) (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

Radian has been operating in the U.S. housing finance system for nearly 50 years (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). For most of that history the company operated as a holding company over a single-line U.S. private mortgage insurer with a small portfolio of adjacent businesses (title, mortgage conduit, real estate services); 2025 ended that single-line orientation.

Two threads explain the trajectory through 2025.

First, **the strategic transformation**. Following a comprehensive strategic review, in September 2025 Radian Group announced both (a) a definitive agreement to acquire Inigo Limited for $1.67 billion in a primarily all-cash transaction, and (b) a planned divestiture of its Mortgage Conduit, Title, and Real Estate Services businesses (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31). The Inigo close on February 2, 2026 was funded entirely from Radian Group's available liquidity sources, including the $600 million Intercompany Note approved by the Pennsylvania Insurance Department; no new equity was raised (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Inigo, launched in 2021, will operate as a stand-alone business unit in London, retaining its CEO Richard Watson, brand, and culture (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). On the divestiture side, the held-for-sale businesses were reclassified to discontinued operations for all periods presented; $62 million of capital was extracted from the held-for-sale entities during Q4 2025 alone, reducing their net carrying value to $110 million at year-end (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Second, **the steady build-up of the mortgage insurance franchise**. Through 2025 the MI book grew to a record $282.5 billion of primary IIF on $55.2 billion of NIW (up from $52.0 billion in 2024), with Q4 2025 NIW of $15.9 billion (up from $13.2 billion in Q4 2024); the in-force premium yield held at 38 basis points; the quarterly persistency rate was 82% in Q4 2025 (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). The reinsurance program has been built layer-by-layer from a sequence of named treaties (2012 through 2027 QSR Agreements; the 2023 and 2025 XOL Agreements) plus the Eagle Re Issuers' insurance-linked-note program; the most recent additions covered $373 million of 2016-2021 policies (Q4 2025) and forward NIW through mid-2028 (the 2025, 2026, and 2027 QSR Agreements) (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Capital return shifted in 2025 to make room for Inigo. During 2025 the company repurchased 13.5 million shares for $430 million; including dividends, total stockholder return for 2025 was $576 million, equivalent to a roughly 8% reduction in share count (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Buybacks were paused in connection with the Inigo close as Radian Group built holdco liquidity to $1.8 billion ahead of the February 2, 2026 closing. Management has indicated that opportunistic share repurchases may resume in the back half of 2026 once liquidity at Radian Group is rebuilt and Radian Guaranty's regulatory dividend approvals support sustainable capital flow (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
