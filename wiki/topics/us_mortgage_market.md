# U.S. Mortgage Market

> The U.S. residential mortgage market is the operating environment for the six U.S. private mortgage insurers. Three structural facts shape MI economics: (1) the GSEs hold or guarantee approximately $7.6 trillion of U.S. single-family and multifamily mortgages — about half of the total U.S. mortgage market — anchoring private mortgage insurance demand to GSE business practices (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024); (2) low-down-payment originations are split among PMI, FHA, VA, and USDA, with the federal channels (FHA + VA + USDA) holding approximately 62% of the low-down-payment market in 2025 vs. 38% for PMI (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31); and (3) the elevated mortgage-rate environment that prevailed since 2022 has supported high MI persistency (industry roughly 82-86% in 2025) but suppressed origination volumes. Industry NIW totaled approximately $311 billion in 2025, $299 billion in 2024, and $284 billion in 2023 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Looking through the cycle, private MI has enabled affordable homeownership for more than 38 million people over more than 65 years of operation, and is the most-used execution for low-down-payment borrowers since 2018 (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

## What it is

**Channels of credit enhancement.** Loans the GSEs purchase with LTV > 80% require credit enhancement under the Enterprises' charter acts (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA; INDUSTRY_FREDDIE_PMI_HANDBOOK_2021-09). The four channels for low-down-payment originations are:

- **Conventional loans with private mortgage insurance** — the segment in which the six MIs compete. Private MI is provided by privately-owned mortgage insurers and is "separate from the insurance or guarantees provided on government-backed loans insured or guaranteed by FHA or VA" (INDUSTRY_FREDDIE_PMI_HANDBOOK_2021-09).
- **FHA-insured** — Federal Housing Administration mortgage insurance, often used for higher-LTV loans and first-time-homebuyer borrowers. The FHA's Mutual Mortgage Insurance ("MMI") Fund had a combined capital ratio of 11.47% for fiscal year 2025, well above the statutory 2% minimum (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **VA-guaranteed** — Department of Veterans Affairs guarantees on loans to qualifying veterans and service members; the VA program offers 100% LTV loans with no down payment (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **USDA-guaranteed** — U.S. Department of Agriculture rural housing program (smaller share) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**Permanent vs. transitory characteristics of private MI.** Private MI begins on day one when the borrower assumes a mortgage, and persists with the lender, GSE, and any mortgage-backed security until cancellation or automatic termination after sufficient equity is established. This is structurally different from government MI, which typically persists for the life of the loan (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11; INDUSTRY_USMI_WHITE_PAPER_2020-10). Cancellation rules are governed by the **Homeowners Protection Act of 1998 ("HOPA")** for borrower-paid mortgage insurance: BPMI must be automatically terminated when LTV (based on original property value and amortized loan balance) reaches 78%, with cancellation upon borrower request when LTV reaches 80% with a "good payment history" (INDUSTRY_FREDDIE_PMI_HANDBOOK_2021-09). LPMI (lender-paid MI) is typically not cancellable.

**Premium structure.** PMI is most commonly paid as monthly premiums, but is also written as annual, single, or split premiums. Borrower-paid MI ("BPMI") is paid by the borrower as part of principal-interest-taxes-and-insurance ("PITI") monthly payments; lender-paid MI ("LPMI") is paid by the lender as a one-time upfront single premium typically built into the mortgage interest rate or the origination fee (INDUSTRY_FREDDIE_PMI_HANDBOOK_2021-09).

**Coverage requirements by LTV range** (Freddie Mac standard, fixed-rate term > 20 years, ARMs, MH):

| LTV ratio | MI Coverage Percentage |
|---|---|
| 80% < LTV ≤ 85% | 12% |
| 85% < LTV ≤ 90% | 25% |
| 90% < LTV ≤ 95% | 30% |
| 95% < LTV ≤ 97% | 35% |

(Source: INDUSTRY_FREDDIE_PMI_HANDBOOK_2021-09. See [[topics/gse_relationship]] for shorter-term and Home Possible product specifics.)

The conforming loan limit for one-unit properties was $806,500 in 2025 (up from $766,550 in 2024); high-cost-area limits were $1,209,750 in 2025 (up from $1,149,825 in 2024) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Three dimensions of the U.S. mortgage market shape MI results most directly.

**First, the FHA / VA / PMI competitive split sets the addressable market**. *Inside Mortgage Finance* estimates that during 2025 the combined low-down-payment market (FHA + VA + USDA + primary PMI) had the following share split: FHA 34.3%, VA 26.8%, USDA roughly 1% (residual), and PMI approximately 38% (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). Combined government-supported channels captured approximately 62.0% of low-down-payment originations in 2025 vs. 58.9% in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). FHA pricing actions (notably the February 2023 30-basis-point reduction in the FHA annual MI premium) and broader FHA capital position create the principal external swing factor for PMI volumes (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Historical share extremes since 2012:
- FHA: low of 23.4% in 2020, high of 42.1% in 2012 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- VA: high of 30.9% in 2020 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

The Urban Institute (2023) characterized PMI as having been "the most common execution for low-down payment borrowers since 2018" (cited in INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

**Second, the interest-rate environment drives both NIW and persistency**. Mortgage rates rose sharply in 2022-2023, suppressing refinance activity and origination volumes; that environment has driven elevated PMI persistency (year-end 2025 12-month persistency ranged from approximately 82% (Arch USMI) to 85.7% (Essent), with most MIs in the 83-85% band). 30-year fixed mortgage rates remained generally above 6 percent in 2024, so the Enterprises' new loan acquisitions were predominantly home purchases rather than refinance activity (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024). As rates eased in late 2024 and through 2025, refinance activity began to pick up; the Q4 2025 quarterly-annualized persistency at Radian (82%) was a small decrease from the prior quarter "due to higher refinance activity" (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Approximately 50% of Radian's IIF and roughly 60% of Essent's IIF carries a mortgage rate of 5.5% or 6% or lower respectively, so the lock-in effect on those vintages remains durable (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**Third, credit normalization is becoming visible in delinquency trends**. Year-end 2025 portfolio delinquency rates: MGIC 2.43% (vs. 2.40% in 2024), Radian 2.56%, Essent 2.50%, Arch USMI 2.17%, Enact (~2-2.4% range), and NMI 1.12%. Industry framing is that the post-COVID delinquency inventory has largely returned to pre-pandemic levels, with the more recent (2022-2024) origination vintages now seasoning into the natural loss-emergence period (3-4 years post-origination); favorable reserve releases on prior-year delinquencies, while still positive, have moderated meaningfully through 2025 (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

The Urban Institute, surveying the 1994-2022 origination period, found that "the loss severity of GSE loans without [private MI] was 37.6 percent, higher than the 26.4 percent severity for loans with [private MI]" — and that "[t]he presence of [private MI] reduces the losses the GSEs experience on loans with LTV ratios above 80 percent to the same levels as the losses they experience on loans with LTV ratios up to 80 percent" (cited in INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

**GSE share of U.S. residential mortgage debt**: Per Federal Reserve data cited in Enact's 10-K, the GSEs held or guaranteed approximately $7.8 trillion as of September 30, 2025 — about 53% of total U.S. 1-4 family residential mortgage debt (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31). Per FHFA, the Enterprises owned or guaranteed approximately $7.6 trillion in single-family and multifamily mortgages at year-end 2024, representing nearly half of the market (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024).

**PMI industry NIW**: $311 billion in 2025 (per MGIC, citing *Inside Mortgage Finance*), $299 billion in 2024, $284 billion in 2023 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). NMI management's estimate for 2025 was approximately $310 billion (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**Total insured mortgage market shares (2025 vs. 2024)**: per *Inside Mortgage Finance* via MGIC and Radian, FHA 34.3% (33.5%), VA 26.8% (24.5%), USDA residual, PMI ~38% (41% in 2024) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

**Conforming loan limits (2025)**: $806,500 for one-unit properties; $1,209,750 in high-cost areas (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**Enterprise SF and MF acquisitions in 2024**: $672 billion combined SF acquisitions (vs. $616 billion in 2023); $120 billion combined MF acquisitions (vs. $101 billion in 2023) (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024). Fannie Mae's 2024 conventional SF acquisition volume was $326.0 billion (vs. $316.0 billion in 2023); Fannie Mae's 2024 MF acquisition volume was $55.1 billion (vs. $52.9 billion in 2023) (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024).

**Mortgage rate environment**: 30-year fixed mortgage rates remained generally above 6 percent throughout 2024 (INDUSTRY_FHFA_ANNUAL_REPORT_2024). Rates rallied during the fourth quarter of 2025, spurring some incremental refinance and purchase activity (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Management commentary across the six MIs in Q4 2025 frames the macro environment as "broadly resilient" with credit performance still benign but with growing focus on the seasoning of the 2022-2024 vintages (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**Industry-level NIW concentrations by share (2025)**: MGIC 19.4% (per *Inside Mortgage Finance*), Radian ~18%, NMI ~15.8% (per its own framing of "near parity"), Essent ~15-16% (per management self-description), with Enact and Arch's USMI making up the balance (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**A new entrant**: Anza Mortgage Insurance Corporation is a potential new entrant into the mortgage insurance industry; based on public disclosures, the entrant intends to begin writing mortgage insurance in 2026 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31). PMIERs requires newly approved insurers to demonstrate at least $500 million of initial capital funding (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 203).

**Industry seriously delinquent rate**: Single-family serious delinquency rates for Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac have stayed below 1% for several years post-crisis (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11), reflecting "the result of enhanced lending standards stemming from the Dodd-Frank Act and implementation of the Ability to Repay/Qualified Mortgage (ATR/QM) requirements" (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

## How it has evolved

The U.S. mortgage market has cycled through three regimes since 2020.

**2020-2021: low-rate / refinance boom**. Mortgage rates fell to historic lows during the COVID-19 monetary policy response, driving a refinance wave that peaked persistency lows of 60.7% at MGIC in March 2021 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). PMI industry NIW reached high levels driven by the surge in originations. In 2019, more than 1.3 million borrowers purchased or refinanced a loan with private MI, accounting for nearly $385 billion in new mortgages (INDUSTRY_USMI_WHITE_PAPER_2020-10). In 2022, more than 1 million borrowers used private MI, accounting for approximately $402 billion in mortgage volume; nearly 62% of purchase loans with private MI went to first-time homebuyers and more than 34% had annual incomes below $75,000 (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11).

**2022-2023: rate-shock / lock-in**. The Federal Reserve's rate hikes in 2022-2023 sharply reduced refinance volumes and origination activity. Persistency surged industry-wide as borrowers locked into low note rates stayed in place. Total PMI industry NIW was $284 billion in 2023, a meaningful decline from earlier-cycle peaks (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). FHA's February 2023 30-basis-point premium reduction further compressed the PMI competitive position (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

**2024-2025: stabilization with modest re-acceleration**. PMI industry NIW rebounded to $299 billion in 2024 and $311 billion in 2025; persistency remained elevated but began softening modestly in 2025 as late-year rate declines spurred incremental refi activity. MGIC's persistency was 85% at year-end 2025; Essent's was 85.7%; Radian's quarterly-annualized persistency was 81.6% at YE 2025 (vs. 82.7% at YE 2024 and 85.8% at YE 2023), with Q4 2025 described by management as "a small decrease from the prior quarter due to higher refinance activity" (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Refinance share rose visibly: refinance-related NIW rose from 4% of MGIC's NIW in 2024 to 9.1% in 2025 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

The natural-disaster overlay (Hurricane Helene in September 2024, Hurricane Milton in October 2024, January 2025 California wildfires, Q4 2025 Hurricane Melissa) introduces episodic delinquency volatility — see [[topics/catastrophe_impact_on_mi]] — without materially changing the secular cycle (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

**Long-run context**: Private MI has enabled affordable homeownership for more than 38 million people over more than 65 years of operation, and has paid nearly $60 billion in claims to the GSEs since they entered conservatorship in 2008 (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11). There are nearly $1.4 trillion in mortgages guaranteed by the GSEs with private MI protection (INDUSTRY_USMI_RESILIENCY_2023-11, citing GSE SEC Filings as of September 30, 2023).

Looking forward, three forces are most likely to shape 2026: (i) the path of mortgage rates and the implied refinance intensity on the 2022-2025 vintages; (ii) any further FHA pricing or programmatic moves; and (iii) the trajectory of GSE recapitalization and conservatorship-release exploration (see [[topics/gse_relationship]]) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
