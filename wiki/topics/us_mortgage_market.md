# U.S. Mortgage Market

> The U.S. residential mortgage market is the operating environment for the six U.S. private mortgage insurers. Three structural facts shape MI economics: the GSEs hold or guarantee approximately $7.8 trillion (~53%) of U.S. 1-4 family residential mortgage debt, anchoring private mortgage insurance demand to GSE business practices; the share of low-down-payment originations split among PMI, FHA, VA, and USDA continues to favor the government channels (FHA + VA + USDA combined was approximately 62.0% of the low-down-payment market in 2025, vs. 58.9% in 2024), with PMI at approximately 38%; and the high-mortgage-rate environment that prevailed since 2022 has supported elevated MI persistency (industry roughly 82-86% in 2025) but suppressed origination volumes. Industry NIW totaled approximately $311 billion in 2025 (vs. $299 billion in 2024 and $284 billion in 2023). The 2025 dynamic was modest market growth combined with the start of a refinance pickup as rates declined into year-end (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

## What it is

The U.S. mortgage market for purposes of MI analysis has four main segments by credit-enhancement channel:

- **Conventional with private mortgage insurance** — loans purchased by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac with PMI satisfying the GSEs' charter requirement to enhance credit on loans with LTVs greater than 80%. This is the segment in which the six MIs compete (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **FHA-insured** — Federal Housing Administration mortgage insurance, typically used for higher-LTV loans and first-time-homebuyer borrowers. The FHA's Mutual Mortgage Insurance ("MMI") Fund had a combined capital ratio of 11.47% for fiscal year 2025, well above the statutory 2% minimum (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **VA-guaranteed** — Department of Veterans Affairs guarantees on loans to qualifying veterans and service members; the VA program offers 100% LTV loans with no down payment (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **USDA-guaranteed** — U.S. Department of Agriculture rural housing program (smaller share) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

In addition, jumbo and non-agency loans (i.e., not conforming to GSE limits, or otherwise not purchased by the GSEs) are originated outside this framework and may carry private MI or other credit-enhancement structures, though these volumes are smaller than the agency-eligible market (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).

The conforming loan limit for one-unit properties was $806,500 in 2025 (up from $766,550 in 2024); high-cost-area limits were $1,209,750 in 2025 (up from $1,149,825 in 2024) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

## Why it matters

Three dimensions of the U.S. mortgage market shape MI results most directly.

First, **the FHA/VA/PMI competitive split sets the addressable market**. *Inside Mortgage Finance* estimates that during 2025, the combined low-down-payment market (FHA + VA + USDA + primary PMI) had the following share split: FHA 34.3%, VA 26.8%, USDA roughly 1% (residual), and PMI approximately 38% — meaning government-supported channels captured approximately 62.0% of low-down-payment originations vs. 58.9% in 2024 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). FHA pricing actions (notably the February 2023 30-basis-point reduction in the FHA annual MI premium) and broader FHA capital position create the principal external swing factor for PMI volumes (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

Historical share extremes since 2012:
- FHA: low of 23.4% in 2020, high of 42.1% in 2012 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- VA: high of 30.9% in 2020 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

Second, **the interest-rate environment drives both NIW and persistency**. Mortgage rates rose sharply in 2022-2023, suppressing refinance activity and origination volumes; that environment has driven elevated PMI persistency (year-end 2025 12-month persistency ranged from approximately 82% (Arch USMI) to 85.7% (Essent), with most MIs in the 83-85% band). As rates eased modestly in late 2024 and through 2025, refinance activity began to pick up; the Q4 2025 quarterly-annualized persistency at Radian (82%) was a small decrease from the prior quarter "due to higher refinance activity" (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Approximately 50% of Radian's IIF and roughly 60% of Essent's IIF carries a mortgage rate of 5.5% or 6% or lower respectively, so the lock-in effect on those vintages remains durable (RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Third, **credit normalization is becoming visible in delinquency trends**. Year-end 2025 portfolio delinquency rates: MGIC 2.43% (vs. 2.40% in 2024), Radian 2.56%, Essent 2.50%, Arch USMI 2.17%, Enact (~2-2.4% range), and NMI 1.12%. Industry framing is that the post-COVID delinquency inventory has largely returned to pre-pandemic levels, with the more recent (2022-2024) origination vintages now seasoning into the natural loss-emergence period (3-4 years post-origination); favorable reserve releases on prior-year delinquencies, while still positive, have moderated meaningfully through 2025 as the post-COVID one-time cushion winds down (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

## Current state (as of 2025-12-31)

- **GSE share of U.S. residential mortgage debt**: Per Federal Reserve data referenced in Enact's 10-K, the GSEs held or guaranteed approximately $7.8 trillion as of September 30, 2025 — about 53% of U.S. 1-4 family residential mortgage debt (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **PMI industry NIW**: $311 billion in 2025 (per MGIC), $299 billion in 2024, $284 billion in 2023 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). NMI management's estimate for 2025 was approximately $310 billion (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **Total insured mortgage market shares (2025 vs. 2024)**: per *Inside Mortgage Finance* via MGIC and Radian, FHA 34.3% (33.5%), VA 26.8% (24.5%), USDA residual, PMI ~38% (41% in 2024) (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Conforming loan limits (2025)**: $806,500 for one-unit properties; $1,209,750 in high-cost areas (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **Mortgage rate environment**: rates rallied during the fourth quarter of 2025, spurring some incremental refinance and purchase activity (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Management commentary across the six MIs in Q4 2025 frames the macro environment as "broadly resilient" with credit performance still benign, but with growing focus on the seasoning of the 2022-2024 vintages (NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **Industry-level NIW concentrations by share (2025)**: MGIC 19.4%, Radian ~18%, NMI ~15.8% (per its own framing of "near parity"), Essent ~15-16% (per management self-description), with Enact and Arch's USMI making up the balance. *Inside Mortgage Finance* is the underlying data source (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31; NMIH_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).
- **PMI share of total insured mortgage market**: approximately 38% in 2025, down from 41% in 2024, per *Inside Mortgage Finance* via Radian (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
- **A new entrant**: Anza Mortgage Insurance Corporation is a potential new entrant into the mortgage insurance industry; based on public disclosures, the entrant intends to begin writing mortgage insurance in 2026 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

The U.S. mortgage market has cycled through three regimes since the 2020 pandemic.

**2020-2021: low-rate / refinance boom**. Mortgage rates fell to historic lows during the COVID-19 monetary policy response, driving a refinance wave that peaked persistency lows of 60.7% at MGIC in March 2021 (MTG_10-K_2024-12-31). PMI industry NIW reached high levels driven by the surge in originations.

**2022-2023: rate-shock / lock-in**. The Federal Reserve's rate hikes in 2022-2023 sharply reduced refinance volumes and origination activity. Persistency surged industry-wide as borrowers locked into low note rates stayed in place. Total PMI industry NIW was $284 billion in 2023, a meaningful decline from earlier-cycle peaks (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31). FHA's February 2023 30-basis-point premium reduction further compressed the PMI competitive position (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

**2024-2025: stabilization with modest re-acceleration**. PMI industry NIW rebounded to $299 billion in 2024 and $311 billion in 2025; persistency remained elevated but began softening modestly in 2025 as late-year rate declines spurred incremental refi activity. MGIC's persistency was 85% at year-end 2025; Essent's was 85.7%; Radian's quarterly-annualized 12-month persistency dropped from 86% in Q3 to 82% in Q4 2025 reflecting the rate rally (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; ESNT_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31). Refinance share rose visibly: refinance-related NIW rose from 4% of MGIC's NIW in 2024 to 9.1% in 2025 (MTG_10-K_2025-12-31).

The natural-disaster overlay (Hurricane Helene, Hurricane Milton, January 2025 California wildfires, Q4 2025 Hurricane Melissa) introduces episodic delinquency volatility — see [[catastrophe_impact_on_mi]] — without materially changing the secular cycle (ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31; ACGL_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31).

Looking forward, three forces are most likely to shape 2026: (i) the path of mortgage rates and the implied refinance intensity on the 2022-2025 vintages; (ii) any further FHA pricing or programmatic moves; and (iii) the trajectory of the GSE recapitalization and conservatorship-release exploration (see [[gse_relationship]]) (MTG_TRANSCRIPT_2025-12-31; RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
