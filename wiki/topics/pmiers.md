# PMIERs

> The Private Mortgage Insurer Eligibility Requirements ("PMIERs") are the coordinated GSE framework that an insurer must meet to be approved to insure loans purchased by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac. PMIERs is administered by the GSEs at the direction of the Federal Housing Finance Agency ("FHFA"), as Conservator of the GSEs. The framework has two layers: a financial test requiring Available Assets to meet or exceed Minimum Required Assets (the greater of $400 million or a risk-based amount calculated from the insurer's adjusted RIF and risk-factor grids), and operational requirements covering quality control, claims processing, master policies, and counterparty conduct (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE). The current base text dates to September 27, 2018 (PMIERs 2.0); two material guidance documents in 2024 — Guidance 2024-01 (issued August 21, 2024, refining Available Assets) and Guidance 2024-02 (effective March 31, 2025, ending the COVID-era extension of the 0.30x multiplier on non-performing loans) — are the most recent significant amendments (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02). At year-end 2025 all six U.S. private mortgage insurers reported PMIERs compliance, with cushions ranging from approximately $1.4-1.6 billion at the lower end (Essent, Radian, NMI) to $2.5 billion at MGIC; Guidance 2024-01 was 50% phased in with the remaining 50% scheduled for March 31 and September 30, 2026.

## What it is

**Origin and purpose.** Mortgage insurance is the most commonly used form of credit enhancement for loans the GSEs purchase with LTVs above 80%; the GSEs' charter acts allow each Enterprise to determine whether a mortgage insurer is qualified to insure loans the Enterprise acquires (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA). Prior to PMIERs, the GSEs had not updated their MI eligibility requirements since 2003 (Fannie Mae) and 2008 (Freddie Mac); the existing requirements relied primarily on rating-agency ratings rather than specific counterparty risk and financial standards (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA). The 2008 financial crisis exposed that gap: MIs and the Enterprises both suffered significant losses from defaults and foreclosures, and three MIs were placed into run-off by their state regulators (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA). FHFA, as Conservator, directed the Enterprises to update, expand, and align their counterparty risk management standards. The first coordinated PMIERs framework was published as a draft for public input on July 10, 2014, finalized on April 17, 2015, and made effective December 31, 2015 (INDUSTRY_USMI_PMIERS_FACTSHEET_2015-10; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA). The current base text is **PMIERs dated September 27, 2018** ("PMIERs 2.0"), effective immediately for new applicants and on March 31, 2019 for existing approved insurers (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE).

The overall test: an authorized officer of the approved insurer must certify quarterly that **Available Assets ≥ Minimum Required Assets** (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 703).

**The financial test.** Available Assets are defined as the most liquid assets of the insurer that are readily available to pay claims; future mortgage guaranty insurance premium revenue is **not** included (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 703). In the 2018 base text, eligible Available Assets are the sum of cash, bonds, common and preferred shares (publicly traded, discounted by 25%, subject to limits), receivables from investments, uncollected premiums, dividends from subsidiaries (under specified ratings conditions), liquid assets at exclusive affiliated reinsurers, lender-captive trust balances, and surrender value of certain Corporate Owned Life Insurance — less unearned premium reserves, debt obligations and pledged collateral, funds held under reinsurance treaties for the benefit of a reinsurer, and ineligible surplus notes (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 703). Bond eligibility and haircuts were materially refined by Guidance 2024-01, which also raised the discount on common and preferred shares from 25% to 50% and added new concentration limits and exclusions (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01 Section 703; see *How it has evolved* below).

**Minimum Required Assets ("MRA")** are the greater of $400 million (a floor that applies even if the risk-based amount would be lower) or the **total risk-based required asset amount** ("RBRAA") calculated under Exhibit A (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 703). The RBRAA uses tables of factors keyed to:

- Original LTV ratio
- Original credit score
- Loan vintage (Pre-2005, 2005-2008, 2009-June 2012, Post June 2012)
- HARP refinance status
- Delinquency or claim status

Performing primary mortgage guaranty insurance is also subject to a **5.6% prudential floor** on performing primary adjusted RIF, ensuring that exposure is supported by a minimum amount of assets regardless of the risk profile (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Exhibit A). For non-Enterprise loans originated after 2008 with high-risk features (no full documentation, non-owner-occupied, DTI > 43%, non-fully-amortizing), multipliers are applied to the grid factors (3.00x / 3.00x / 2.00x / 1.50x respectively) (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA). The macroeconomic stress underpinning the grids is the Federal Reserve Board's Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review ("CCAR") Severely Adverse scenario for post-2008 loans, and the CCAR Baseline scenario for pre-2009 loans, with national paths disaggregated to state level and extended to 30 years (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA).

**Reinsurance credit (Section 707).** An approved insurer that cedes risk to a reinsurer may receive a reduction in its RBRAA, conditional on Fannie Mae's written approval and the reinsurer meeting counterparty financial requirements (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707). The collateral required to be pledged in trust is set by Table 707-1, which scales with the reinsurer's average S&P / A.M. Best / Moody's rating; below-investment-grade reinsurers face a 75% required collateral percentage (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707). Eligible collateral is limited to USD cash, U.S. Treasuries, U.S. Federal Agency Securities (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, Ginnie Mae MBS), specified money market funds, and certain investment-grade corporate debt (capped at 30% of the trust account) (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707). A counterparty risk haircut is also applied against the uncollateralized portion of the ceded amount (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707, Table 707-2). For risk ceded to special purpose vehicles in reinsurance securitization structures (e.g., the insurance-linked-note structures used by MGIC's Home Re Entities and Radian's Eagle Re Issuers), Fannie Mae may permit a reduction in the RBRAA in an amount it determines (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707).

**Operational requirements.** PMIERs covers a wide span of operational matters, including a documented risk diversification policy, a formal Quality Control program (loans selected with at least a 95% confidence interval and a 2% margin of error, plus 100% review of early payment defaults), claims processing within 180 days of perfection, master-policy compliance, and Fidelity Bond / Errors & Omissions insurance with minimum coverage of $5 million per policy (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE).

**Newly-approved insurer requirements.** A new entrant must demonstrate at least $500 million of initial capital funding, must subsequently maintain at least $400 million of Available Assets, and is subject to additional restrictions for its first three years (notably, prohibition on dividends or capital movements to affiliates without GSE approval) (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 203; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA).

## Why it matters

Three reasons PMIERs is the central regulatory artifact for U.S. private mortgage insurance.

**First, compliance is binary on the asset side.** An MI must remain PMIERs-compliant to write new business on loans the GSEs will acquire. Loss of GSE eligibility would have a material adverse effect on any of the six MIs' business (ACT_10-K_2025-12-31; ESNT_10-K_2025-12-31). The PMIERs cushion (Available Assets in excess of Minimum Required Assets) is therefore the most-watched capital figure across the industry, more important operationally than state-level RTC ratios (which most state regulators cap at 25:1 — typically less binding than PMIERs).

**Second, PMIERs amendments shift required capital in non-trivial ways.** Three classes of amendment have been material in recent years:

- **Bond eligibility and haircuts** (Guidance 2024-01): tighter rules on which bonds qualify as Available Assets, with limits on residential mortgage-backed and commercial real estate-backed assets to mitigate the risk that an MI's investment portfolio loses value at the same time housing stress increases claims (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01).
- **Disaster and forbearance multipliers**: A 0.30x multiplier on the RBRAA factor for non-performing loans backed by properties in FEMA Declared Major Disaster Areas eligible for individual assistance — applied for up to three calendar months after the loan reaches two missed payments, or longer if subject to a forbearance plan (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02). A separate COVID-era extension applied the same 0.30x multiplier to non-performing loans resulting from COVID-19 hardship; that extension was discontinued effective March 31, 2025 (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02).
- **Reinsurance credit framework**: Section 707 is the rulebook for how much of an MI's RBRAA can be reduced for ceded risk, and changes in the table of required collateral percentages or counterparty haircuts ripple directly into the cushion (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Section 707).

**Third, the rule is administered with discretion.** Both Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac retain the right, in their sole discretion, to modify, waive, or amend any provision of PMIERs, and have broad discretion to interpret it (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01). This means the GSEs can issue interim guidance, raise the Minimum Required Assets for an individual approved insurer where they determine the insurer has retained risk beyond traditional primary or pool MI, or impose remediation actions ranging from heightened reporting to suspension or termination of approved-insurer status (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE Sections 703 and 901). The GSEs frequently evaluate PMIERs for additional interim changes (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).

## How it has evolved

The PMIERs framework has been a decade-long arc from rating-agency-based eligibility to a coordinated, codified, financial-and-operational standard.

**2003 / 2008**: Original Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac mortgage insurer eligibility requirements published separately. Each Enterprise relied primarily on acceptable rating-agency ratings rather than specific counterparty risk and financial standards (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA).

**2008-2009 financial crisis**: Significant defaults and foreclosures of Enterprise loans drive material losses to MIs and to the Enterprises. Three MIs are placed in run-off by their state regulators (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA).

**July 10, 2014**: FHFA publishes draft revised PMIERs for public input (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA; INDUSTRY_USMI_PMIERS_FACTSHEET_2015-10).

**April 17, 2015**: FHFA releases final PMIERs (INDUSTRY_USMI_PMIERS_FACTSHEET_2015-10).

**December 31, 2015**: Original PMIERs effective. Pre-PMIERs MI capital requirements had been state-defined as a multiple of risk insured (commonly 25:1) with no coordinated GSE financial test; the new PMIERs introduces the Available Assets / Minimum Required Assets framework with a $400 million floor (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_OVERVIEW_FHFA; INDUSTRY_USMI_PMIERS_FACTSHEET_2015-10).

**September 27, 2018**: PMIERs 2.0 published — the current base text. Effective immediately for new applicants and on March 31, 2019 for existing approved insurers (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_2.0_BASE).

**June 30, 2020**: PMIERs guidance issued (subsequently codified in PMIERs Guidance 2021-01, dated June 30, 2021, and later superseded by Guidance 2024-02). Introduces the 0.30x multiplier on the RBRAA factor for non-performing loans (i) backed by properties in FEMA Declared Major Disaster Areas eligible for individual assistance, with three-month application from the time the loan reaches two missed payments or longer if subject to a forbearance plan, and (ii) extended for COVID-19 hardship-related non-performing loans during a defined COVID-19 Crisis Period (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02 Appendix A).

**June 30, 2020 - December 31, 2021**: Section 705 (Limitations Triggered by an Available Assets Shortfall) is temporarily amended to require Freddie Mac's prior written approval before paying dividends or moving capital, with thresholds tied to Available Assets surplus (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02 Appendix A).

**April 10, 2023**: COVID-19 National Emergency, declared on March 13, 2020, ends (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02 Appendix A).

**August 21, 2024**: GSEs and FHFA release **PMIERs Guidance 2024-01**, amending Section 703 (Available Assets and Minimum Required Assets), Section 802 (Reporting), and Exhibit D, and introducing Exhibit H. The substantive changes refine bond eligibility and haircuts:

- Bonds must be rated by S&P, Moody's, Fitch, A.M. Best, DBRS, or KBRA; unrated bonds are ineligible (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01 Section 703).
- A new haircut table by rating: 0.20% for AAA, 0.60% for AA+ to AA-, 1.30% for A+ to A-, 2.10% for BBB+ to BBB-, 10.00% for BB+ to BB- (with 5-year maximum remaining term), 25.00% for B+ to B- (with 2-year maximum remaining term), 100% for CCC+ and below; full faith and credit U.S. Government securities have a 0% haircut (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01 Section 703).
- Exclusions: any mortgage insurance-linked note (MILN), all non-agency residential mortgage-backed securities, BB-rated bonds with terms over 5 years, B-rated bonds with terms over 2 years, all bonds rated CCC+ or below, and non-agency CMBS rated below BBB- (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01 Section 703).
- Concentration limits: GSE debt/MBS ≤ 25% of Available Assets; ABS ≤ 20%; sub-investment-grade bonds + common/preferred shares aggregate ≤ 5%; non-agency CMBS ≤ 5%; eligible surplus notes capped at 9% of Minimum Required Assets; COLI ≤ 10% of risk-based required asset amount (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01 Section 703).

**Guidance 2024-02** (effective March 31, 2025): Replaces 2021 Guidance, in light of the COVID-19 National Emergency having ended. Restates Table 8 footnote 1 for the FEMA Major Disaster Area 0.30x multiplier (still in effect), and discontinues the COVID-era extension of that multiplier (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02). Adds a new defined term "Missed Monthly Payment" in the glossary and updates Section 802 reporting requirements for Exhibit D and Exhibit F (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02 Section II and III).

**March 31, 2025**: Both Guidance 2024-01 and 2024-02 take effect. The first phase-in milestone of 2024-01 is implemented (75% of the calculated adjustment is added back, i.e., 25% of the new rules are in effect). The COVID-era 0.30x multiplier extension is discontinued (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01; INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-02).

**September 30, 2025**: Second phase-in milestone (50% phased in) (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01).

**March 31, 2026**: Third phase-in milestone (75% phased in) (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01).

**September 30, 2026**: Final phase-in milestone (100% phased in). Guidance 2024-01 will be incorporated into the next published version of PMIERs (INDUSTRY_PMIERS_GUIDANCE_2024-01).

The trajectory of PMIERs since 2015 has been one of steady codification (2015 → 2018 PMIERs 2.0), event-driven accommodations (the 2020 disaster and COVID guidance), and incremental tightening of the asset side (the 2024 Available Assets refinements). The GSEs have signaled they expect to continue updating the framework as needed (RDN_10-K_2025-12-31).
